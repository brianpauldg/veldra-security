## Before You Begin

Gather the following supplies before starting:

- [ ] TB-500 vial (reconstituted — check expiration date and reconstitution date)
- [ ] Insulin syringe: **29-gauge to 31-gauge, 5/16" (8mm), 0.5mL or 1mL barrel**
- [ ] Alcohol swabs (2 — one for the vial top, one for the injection site)
- [ ] Sterile gauze pad
- [ ] FDA-cleared sharps container
- [ ] Clean, flat surface with good lighting

**Before every injection:**

1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
2. Inspect the vial — the reconstituted solution should be clear and colorless
3. Check the expiration date and reconstitution date on the vial
4. If the medication has been stored in the refrigerator, it may be injected cold or allowed to reach room temperature

**If something does not look right, do not inject. Contact your care team.**

---

## Reconstitution

TB-500 is typically supplied as a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder requiring reconstitution with bacteriostatic water:

1. Clean the tops of both vials (TB-500 powder and bacteriostatic water) with alcohol swabs
2. Using a syringe, draw the volume of bacteriostatic water specified by your compounding pharmacy
3. Inject the bacteriostatic water slowly into the TB-500 vial, directing the stream against the glass wall
4. **Gently swirl** until fully dissolved. **Do not shake.**
5. The solution should be clear and colorless. If cloudy or particulate, do not use.
6. Write the reconstitution date on the vial label
7. Refrigerate immediately after reconstitution
8. Use within the timeframe specified by your compounding pharmacy

## Identifying Your Syringe

**This protocol uses an insulin syringe.** This is a small syringe with a fixed, very thin needle.

## Injection Sites

### Recommended Sites

**Abdomen**
The fatty tissue of the abdomen, at least 2 inches from the navel. Rotate within the abdominal region.

**Anterior Thigh**
The front of the thigh, middle third between the knee and hip.

**Upper Arm**
The back of the upper arm, between the shoulder and elbow. May require assistance.

## Step-by-Step Injection Technique

### Drawing Medication

1. Clean the vial top with an alcohol swab
2. Draw air equal to your prescribed dose volume into the syringe
3. Insert needle into vial, inject air, invert vial
4. Draw your prescribed dose
5. Check for and expel air bubbles
6. Remove needle from vial

### Injecting

7. Clean injection site with alcohol swab — allow to air dry
8. **Pinch a fold of skin** at the injection site
9. Insert needle at **90 degrees** into the pinched fold
10. Inject slowly over 5–10 seconds
11. Wait 5 seconds before withdrawing
12. Withdraw needle, release skin pinch
13. Apply gentle pressure with gauze — do not rub

### After Injection

14. Dispose of syringe in sharps container immediately
15. Record in rotation tracker

## What to Expect After Injection

**Normal:** Small drop of blood, slight redness, mild injection site firmness resolving within days.

**Contact your care team:** Worsening redness/swelling beyond 3 days, drainage, fever, allergic reaction symptoms.

---

## Troubleshooting

**Blood after injection.** Normal — apply gentle pressure.

**Bent needle.** Discard and use new syringe.

**Missed dose.** Administer when remembered. Do not double dose. Contact care team if unsure.

**Site reaction beyond 3 days.** Contact care team.

## Storage and Disposal

- **Refrigerate after reconstitution** at 36–46°F (2–8°C)
- **Do not freeze**
- Use within the timeframe specified by your compounding pharmacy
- Protect from light
- Dispose of needles/syringes in FDA-cleared sharps container
- Return unused medication to pharmacy
