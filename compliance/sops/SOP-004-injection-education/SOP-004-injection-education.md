## 1. Purpose

This SOP establishes the framework, governance, and clinical content authority for all patient-facing self-administration injection education materials produced by Bloom Metabolics. This SOP governs:

- The clinical injection protocols by which patients self-administer prescribed injectable medications
- The patient-facing injection instruction documents in three formats: DOCX print-ready packaging inserts, PDF downloadable handouts, and Markdown source for website rendering
- The web-rendered protocol pages on bloommetabolics.com accessed via QR codes printed on medication packaging
- The OptiMantra acknowledgment workflow by which patients confirm receipt and understanding of injection instructions
- The version control, review cycle, and clinical authority workflow for protocol revisions

The clinical content of the protocol attachments instructs patients on how to administer prescription medications via injection. This is unambiguously clinical content under [SOP-001 §2.3](../SOP-001-document-control/SOP-001-document-control.md) and rests with PC ownership and Covering Physician approval.

The MSO Owner (Brian DeGuzman, RN) operationally delegates drafting, formatting, version control, and operational implementation under the operational delegation provision of SOP-001 §2.3. Content authority remains with the PC. Final approval of all clinical content requires Covering Physician sign-off.

## 3. Definitions

**Self-Administration**
The act of a patient injecting a prescribed medication into their own body without direct clinical supervision. Self-administration is the standard delivery model for Bloom Metabolics injectable medications, as the practice operates via telehealth.

**Injection Protocol**
A structured clinical document that instructs a patient on how to safely self-administer a specific injectable medication. Each protocol covers syringe identification, injection sites, needle angle and technique, site rotation, troubleshooting, and escalation. Protocols are attached to this SOP as numbered attachments.

**Protocol Attachment**
A formal attachment to this SOP containing a complete injection protocol. Each protocol attachment exists in three formats (Markdown, DOCX, PDF) derived from a single Markdown source. Protocol attachments are numbered sequentially (Protocol 001, Protocol 002, etc.).

**Provisional Status**
A time-limited approval status defined in [SOP-001 §5.6](../SOP-001-document-control/SOP-001-document-control.md). A Provisional protocol is operationally usable but has not undergone full clinical review. Provisional status expires 180 calendar days from initial approval and must be resolved by full approval, re-approval with documented exception, or retirement.

**Acknowledgment of Receipt (Patient-Facing)**
The electronic acknowledgment completed by a patient in OptiMantra confirming that they have reviewed and understood the injection protocol(s) for their prescribed medication(s). The acknowledgment is captured as part of the clinical record and is required before first self-administration.

**Site Rotation**
The practice of varying injection locations between administrations to allow tissue recovery and prevent complications such as lipohypertrophy, fibrosis, or abscess formation.

**Lipohypertrophy**
A localized increase in subcutaneous fat tissue caused by repeated injection at the same site. Lipohypertrophy can alter medication absorption and is prevented by consistent site rotation.

**Aspiration**
The practice of pulling back on the syringe plunger after needle insertion to check for blood return before injecting. Current evidence-based practice does not support routine aspiration for intramuscular injections in the recommended sites or for subcutaneous injections. The Covering Physician may modify this guidance.

**Subcutaneous (SubQ)**
An injection route delivering medication into the fatty tissue layer between the skin and muscle. SubQ injections use shorter, thinner needles (typically 29–31 gauge, 5/16" to 1/2" length) and a 90-degree angle with skin pinch or 45-degree angle for very lean patients.

**Intramuscular (IM)**
An injection route delivering medication into muscle tissue. IM injections use longer, larger-gauge needles (typically 23–25 gauge, 1" length) and a 90-degree angle without skin pinch.

**Insulin Syringe**
A small-volume syringe (typically 0.5mL or 1mL) with a fixed, non-detachable needle designed for subcutaneous injection. Used for SubQ protocols in this SOP.

**Intramuscular Syringe**
A larger-volume syringe (typically 1mL or 3mL) with a detachable needle designed for intramuscular injection. Allows the use of a separate draw needle and injection needle. Used for IM protocols in this SOP.

**Pinch-and-Poke Technique**
A subcutaneous injection technique in which the patient pinches a fold of skin at the injection site to lift subcutaneous tissue away from muscle, then inserts the needle into the pinched fold. Used for SubQ protocols to ensure medication is deposited in subcutaneous tissue rather than muscle.

**Service Line**
A category of clinical services offered by Bloom Metabolics. Injectable service lines at launch: Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT), GLP-1/GIP Weight Management (semaglutide, tirzepatide), and adjunctive HCG therapy. Future injectable service lines pending LegitScript certification: peptide therapy (BPC-157, TB-500).

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Protocol Authorship and Initial Provisional Approval

The MSO Owner drafts protocol attachments using current evidence-based parameters consistent with:

- FDA prescribing information for the relevant medication
- AUA Clinical Guidelines on Testosterone Deficiency (for TRT protocols)
- Endocrine Society Clinical Practice Guidelines
- Obesity Medicine Association guidelines (for GLP-1/GIP protocols)
- Current peer-reviewed clinical literature on injection technique
- Standard nursing references for patient self-administration education

The drafted protocols are submitted to the Covering Physician for initial Provisional review and approval. Initial Provisional approval requires:

1. Covering Physician review of clinical content in all protocol attachments
2. Covering Physician signature on the SOP-004 approval page (§12 of this document)
3. Documentation of any modifications requested by the Covering Physician, with revisions applied before signature

Upon initial Provisional approval, the SOP and its protocol attachments enter the 180-day Provisional clock per [SOP-001 §5.6](../SOP-001-document-control/SOP-001-document-control.md). During the Provisional period, protocols are operationally usable and distributed to patients with the PROVISIONAL CLINICAL CONTENT disclosure visible on every artifact.

### 5.2 Full Clinical Review and Transition to Approved

Within 180 calendar days of initial Provisional approval, the Covering Physician conducts a full clinical review of each protocol attachment. Full review includes:

- Validation of all clinical parameters against current FDA prescribing information
- Validation against current clinical guidelines applicable to the medication and route of administration
- Review of any patient-reported issues, near-misses, or adverse events occurring during the Provisional period
- Review of any FDA safety communications, label changes, or guideline updates issued during the Provisional period
- Review of patient acknowledgment completion rates and any patterns in patient questions suggesting documentation gaps

Full review concludes with one of three outcomes per [SOP-001 §5.6](../SOP-001-document-control/SOP-001-document-control.md):

- **(a) Complete approval** — protocol transitions to Approved status; PROVISIONAL CLINICAL CONTENT disclosure is removed from all formats
- **(b) Re-approval as Provisional** with documented exception rationale — extends Provisional status by one additional 180-day period (total maximum 360 days from initial approval)
- **(c) Retire** — protocol is withdrawn and the medication ceases to be offered with self-administration education until a new compliant protocol is developed

### 5.3 Protocol Format Generation

Each protocol exists as a single Markdown source of truth. From the Markdown source, the MSO Owner generates:

- **DOCX** — print-ready medication packaging insert via `compliance/build/build-docx.mjs`
- **PDF** — patient-facing downloadable handout via `compliance/build/build-pdf.mjs`
- **Web HTML** — rendered on bloommetabolics.com via the website build pipeline

All three formats are derived from the same Markdown source. Format generation is operational; clinical content authority remains with the Covering Physician. The MSO Owner verifies format fidelity after each generation cycle.

### 5.4 QR Code Routing and Medication Packaging

Each medication shipped to a patient includes a printed packaging insert (the DOCX-derived print-ready document) and a QR code routing to the corresponding web-rendered protocol page on bloommetabolics.com.

### 5.5 OptiMantra Acknowledgment Workflow

Before first self-administration, every active patient acknowledges receipt and understanding of the protocol(s) for their prescribed medication(s). The acknowledgment captures:

- Patient identifier (OptiMantra patient ID)
- Protocol(s) acknowledged with version number
- Timestamp
- Patient electronic signature
- Affirmation statement: *"I have reviewed the injection instructions for [medication]. I understand the technique, the injection sites, the rotation schedule, and the signs of complications. I will contact my Bloom Metabolics care team via the patient portal with any questions before injecting."*

Acknowledgment is captured in OptiMantra as part of the clinical record. No patient may proceed to first self-administration without documented acknowledgment. The acknowledgment record is referenced in the patient's chart and is available to the Covering Physician.

### 5.6 Protocol Distribution at Medication Shipment

When a medication is shipped to a patient:

1. The compounding pharmacy includes the printed protocol insert (DOCX-derived) in the shipping package
2. The shipping confirmation email includes a link to the protocol's web page
3. The OptiMantra patient portal displays a notification prompting the patient to review the protocol and complete acknowledgment
4. If acknowledgment is not completed within 7 days of shipment, an automated reminder is sent via OptiMantra
5. If acknowledgment is not completed within 14 days of shipment, the MSO Owner is notified for direct outreach to the patient

### 5.7 Protocol Revision Triggers

A protocol revision is initiated when any of the following occur:

- FDA prescribing information update for the medication
- FDA safety communication affecting injection technique or contraindications
- Updated clinical guideline from a relevant professional society (AUA, Endocrine Society, Obesity Medicine Association, etc.)
- Patient-reported issue, near-miss, or adverse event implicating the protocol content
- Compounding pharmacy formulation change affecting injection volume, concentration, or storage requirements
- Cumulative patient questions about a specific aspect of the protocol suggesting a documentation gap
- Annual scheduled review per [SOP-001 §5.10](../SOP-001-document-control/SOP-001-document-control.md)

Revision workflow follows [SOP-001 §5.11](../SOP-001-document-control/SOP-001-document-control.md) (minor or major revision per impact assessment). All protocol revisions require Covering Physician approval before distribution to patients.

### 5.8 Format Synchronization

When a protocol is revised, all three formats (Markdown, DOCX, PDF) and the web-rendered page are regenerated from the updated Markdown source. The MSO Owner verifies synchronization within 7 calendar days of approved revision:

1. Regenerate DOCX from updated Markdown via `build-docx.mjs`
2. Regenerate PDF from updated DOCX via `build-pdf.mjs`
3. Deploy updated Markdown to website build pipeline
4. Verify all three formats display identical clinical content
5. Log synchronization verification in the protocol's revision history

Existing patients whose prescribed medication protocol has undergone a material change receive notification of the update via OptiMantra with a prompt to review the revised protocol.

## 7. Escalation & Exception Handling

### 7.1 Patient Inability to Self-Administer

If a patient reports inability to self-administer after reviewing the protocol:

1. MSO staff documents the patient's concern in OptiMantra
2. The Covering Physician is notified via OptiMantra for clinical guidance
3. The Covering Physician may: (a) schedule a synchronous telehealth visit for real-time injection coaching, (b) recommend the patient visit a local clinic or pharmacy for the first administration with guidance, or (c) modify the treatment plan to a non-injectable formulation where clinically appropriate
4. The resolution is documented in the patient's OptiMantra record

### 7.2 Patient Reports of Injection Complications During Provisional Period

Injection complications reported during the Provisional period receive heightened scrutiny:

1. The patient's report is documented in OptiMantra
2. The MSO Owner notifies the Covering Physician within 1 business day
3. The Covering Physician evaluates whether the complication is: (a) consistent with expected injection site reactions, (b) suggestive of a technique error that may implicate the protocol content, or (c) an adverse event requiring formal reporting
4. If the protocol content is implicated, the Covering Physician initiates a protocol revision per §5.7
5. All Provisional-period complications are included in the full clinical review at §5.2

### 7.3 FDA Safety Communication Requiring Immediate Protocol Freeze

If an FDA safety communication is issued that directly affects the injection technique or contraindications for a medication covered by a protocol:

1. The MSO Owner immediately suspends distribution of the affected protocol (removes from website, halts packaging insert printing)
2. The Covering Physician reviews the safety communication within 2 business days
3. The Covering Physician determines whether the protocol must be revised, suspended, or retired
4. Distribution resumes only after the Covering Physician authorizes the revised or unchanged protocol
5. Patients who received the affected protocol before the freeze are notified of the safety communication via OptiMantra

### 7.4 Compounding Pharmacy Formulation Change

If a compounding pharmacy partner notifies Bloom Metabolics of a formulation change (concentration, vehicle, storage requirements) affecting a medication covered by a protocol:

1. The MSO Owner updates the affected protocol's storage and preparation sections
2. The revision is routed to the Covering Physician for approval per §5.7
3. If the formulation change affects injection volume or technique (not just storage), the revision is classified as a major revision per [SOP-001 §5.11.2](../SOP-001-document-control/SOP-001-document-control.md)
4. Updated protocols are distributed per §5.6 and §5.8

### 7.5 Near-Miss Requiring Clinical Review

A near-miss is any event in which a patient reports a situation that could have resulted in harm but did not (e.g., patient started to inject with the wrong syringe type but stopped and contacted the care team). Near-miss handling:

1. Document the near-miss in OptiMantra
2. MSO Owner reviews the near-miss for operational factors (unclear protocol language, confusing syringe packaging)
3. Covering Physician reviews for clinical factors (technique confusion, injection site error)
4. If the near-miss implicates the protocol content, initiate revision per §5.7
5. Aggregate near-miss data is included in the annual protocol review

## 8. Training & Competency

### 8.1 Covering Physician

The Covering Physician's competency to review and approve injection protocols is assumed by virtue of their medical training, licensure, and clinical experience. No additional SOP-specific training is required for the Covering Physician beyond reading this SOP and its protocol attachments.

### 8.2 MSO Owner and MSO Staff

MSO personnel responsible for protocol distribution and acknowledgment management must complete:

1. Reading of this SOP (complete) with signed acknowledgment
2. Reading of all active protocol attachments
3. Operational training on OptiMantra acknowledgment workflow configuration
4. Operational training on DOCX/PDF format generation and website deployment
5. Training on the data classification boundary: MSO staff distribute protocols and manage acknowledgments but do NOT provide clinical guidance on injection technique. Patient questions about technique are routed to the Covering Physician.

### 8.3 Patient Education

No formal patient-side training program is required beyond the protocol content itself. The protocol attachments are designed to be self-contained educational documents. The OptiMantra acknowledgment workflow (§5.5) serves as the verification that the patient has reviewed and understood the protocol. Patients who express difficulty understanding the protocol are escalated per §7.1.
