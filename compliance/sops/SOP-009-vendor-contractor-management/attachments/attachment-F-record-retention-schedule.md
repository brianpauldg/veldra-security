## Purpose

This attachment defines the retention periods for all vendor and contractor records maintained under SOP-009. Bloom Metabolics' standard retention periods exceed IRS and HIPAA minimums to provide adequate audit defense and to align with corporate record retention best practices.

## Retention Period Rationale

**Why 7 years (Bloom standard)?**

- The IRS generally has 3 years from the filing date to audit a return, but this extends to 6 years if gross income is understated by more than 25%, and there is no statute of limitations for fraud. A 7-year retention period provides adequate coverage for the extended audit window and aligns with common corporate record retention practices.
- California's statute of limitations on written contracts is 4 years (CCP § 337). A 7-year retention period provides a comfortable margin.
- The California Employment Development Department (EDD) can audit payroll tax compliance for up to 3 years after the return due date, with extensions for fraud or failure to file. A 7-year retention of classification analyses supports audit defense.

**Why 6 years for BAAs?**

- HIPAA requires retention of BAAs and related documentation for 6 years from the date of creation or last effective date (45 CFR § 164.530(j)). Bloom applies the 6-year HIPAA minimum unless the 7-year corporate standard produces a later destruction date (e.g., if the engagement ends 1 year after the BAA is created, the BAA is retained for 7 years post-engagement-end, which exceeds the 6-year HIPAA minimum).

**Why 3 years for email correspondence?**

- Material email correspondence supports audit defense and dispute resolution, but the volume of email makes indefinite retention impractical. Three years post-engagement-end covers the primary audit and statute-of-limitations windows while keeping the archive manageable.

## Destruction Procedure

When a document's retention period has expired:

1. **Identify expired records.** The bookkeeper runs an annual review (at the start of each calendar year) to identify vendor records that have reached the end of their retention period.
2. **Confirm eligibility.** The MSO Owner confirms that no active litigation, audit, investigation, or regulatory inquiry affects the records. If any such proceeding is active or reasonably anticipated, the records are placed on litigation hold and are NOT destroyed until the hold is lifted.
3. **Destroy securely.** Paper records are shredded (cross-cut). Digital records are permanently deleted from all storage locations (vendor file, AP tool, archive, backup).
4. **Document destruction.** The bookkeeper logs the destruction in the SOP-009 records: vendor name, document types destroyed, retention period applied, destruction date, and method. The destruction log is retained permanently.

## State-Specific Notes

- **California** may impose additional retention requirements for specific document types. The MSO Owner reviews retention periods with the CPA annually to confirm compliance with current California law.
- If Bloom expands to additional states, state-specific retention requirements must be evaluated and the retention schedule updated.
