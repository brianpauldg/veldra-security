# Attachment D — Pre-Intake Inquiry Scripts

**Parent Document:** [SOP-002 — Patient Intake Operations](../SOP-002-patient-intake-operations.md)

---

## Overview

This attachment provides approved scripted responses for pre-intake inquiries — any contact from a prospective patient who has not yet completed the full intake process. The governing rule from SOP-002 §5.1 applies: MSO staff do not provide clinical advice, clinical opinions, or representations about treatment efficacy or appropriateness.

**When in doubt, use the universal deferral:** "That's a clinical question that our Medical Director will address after reviewing your complete intake. Would you like me to send you the intake link?"

---

## Category 1 — Clinical Questions

These inquiries ask about diagnoses, treatments, medications, or medical conditions. The response ALWAYS defers to the Medical Director and the completed intake.

| Inquiry Pattern | Approved Response | What NOT to Say |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "Can you treat [condition]?" / "Do you treat [condition]?" | "We provide telehealth services for hormone optimization, weight management, sexual health, and longevity treatments. Whether a specific treatment is appropriate for you is a clinical decision made by our Medical Director after reviewing your complete intake. The intake takes about 15 minutes. Would you like the link?" | Do not confirm or deny that any specific condition is treatable. Do not name specific medications. |
| "Can you treat me?" / "Am I eligible?" | "We provide telehealth services to California residents. Eligibility is determined by our Medical Director after a complete intake. The intake includes a clinical evaluation fee. Would you like the intake link?" | Do not say "yes" or "probably." Do not pre-qualify the patient. |
| "What testosterone dose would I get?" / "What dose of semaglutide?" | "Specific dosing is a clinical decision made by our Medical Director after reviewing your full intake, medical history, and labs. I can't speak to specific dosing — that's determined individually for each patient." | Do not quote dosage ranges, typical doses, or starting doses. |
| "I take [medication X], is that a problem?" / "I have [condition Y], can I still do this?" | "That's a clinical question I can't answer without our Medical Director reviewing your full medical history. Our intake captures all medications and allergies, and the Medical Director reviews for any interactions or contraindications. Would you like the intake link?" | Do not say "that should be fine" or "that might be an issue." Do not name specific contraindications. |
| "Will my labs be okay?" / "My testosterone is [number], is that low?" | "Lab interpretation is part of our clinical evaluation. I can't speak to your specific lab values — that's for the Medical Director to assess after reviewing your complete intake." | Do not interpret lab values. Do not state normal ranges. |
| "Is TRT safe?" / "Are there side effects?" | "All medical treatments carry considerations that our Medical Director will discuss with you as part of your evaluation. I'm not qualified to provide clinical information on specific treatments." | Do not list side effects. Do not make safety claims. |
| "How long until I see results?" | "Treatment timelines are individual and are discussed by the Medical Director during your clinical evaluation. I can't make specific predictions." | Do not quote typical timelines. |

## Category 2 — Dosing and Medication-Specific Questions

| Inquiry Pattern | Approved Response | What NOT to Say |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "What medications do you prescribe?" | "We offer treatments for hormone optimization (TRT), weight management, sexual health, and longevity. The specific medications prescribed for you would be determined by our Medical Director based on your clinical evaluation." | Do not list specific drug names unprompted. If the patient names a drug, do not confirm or deny its availability. |
| "Do you prescribe [specific drug]?" | "Our Medical Director determines appropriate treatments based on your individual evaluation. I can't confirm specific medications without a clinical review. Would you like to start the intake?" | Do not confirm drug availability. Do not say "yes, we prescribe that." |
| "Can I get [specific drug] without labs?" | "Our Medical Director requires appropriate clinical information, which may include laboratory work, before making prescribing decisions. The specifics are determined during your evaluation." | Do not say labs are or are not required for specific drugs. |

I'd recommend starting the intake to see if we're a good fit for your goals." | Do not claim to be "better." Do not cite competitor weaknesses. |

If you'd like to proceed, the intake process includes a clinical evaluation by our Medical Director." | Do not cite certifications not yet obtained (e.g., LegitScript pending). State facts only. |

## Category 4 — Payment and Insurance Questions

| Inquiry Pattern | Approved Response | What NOT to Say |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "Do you take insurance?" | "Bloom Metabolics operates on a cash-pay basis. We do not bill insurance directly. We can provide a superbill (detailed receipt) that you may submit to your insurance for potential reimbursement, but reimbursement is not guaranteed." | Do not make promises about insurance reimbursement. |
| "How much does it cost?" | "The initial clinical evaluation fee is $[FEE]. Treatment costs vary based on the individualized plan developed by our Medical Director. Specific pricing for ongoing treatment is discussed during your clinical evaluation." | Do not quote medication prices unless published on the website. Do not estimate total treatment cost. |
| "Can I get a refund?" | "Our refund policy is outlined in our Terms of Service, which you'll review during the intake process. If you have a specific concern, I'm happy to have someone follow up with you." | Do not make refund commitments outside the Terms of Service. |

## Category 5 — Timeline and Process Questions

| Inquiry Pattern | Approved Response | What NOT to Say |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "How long does the process take?" | "The intake form takes about 15 minutes. After completing intake and payment, our team reviews your information, and you'll typically be scheduled for a clinical evaluation within [X business days]. Exact timelines depend on our current patient volume." | Do not promise specific turnaround times not yet established. |
| "When can I get my prescription?" | "Prescriptions are issued by our Medical Director after completing the clinical evaluation, which follows your intake. The timeline depends on your individual situation, including whether additional labs are needed." | Do not promise prescriptions. Do not estimate prescription timelines. |
| "Can I talk to a doctor first?" | "Our clinical evaluations begin after a completed intake. This ensures the Medical Director has your full medical history before your conversation. The intake is the first step." | Do not arrange pre-intake physician calls. No clinical contact before intake completion. |
| "Can I just do a discovery call?" | "We don't offer discovery calls prior to intake. The intake process is designed to give our Medical Director everything needed for your evaluation. It takes about 15 minutes to complete." | Do not offer pre-intake discovery calls. |

## Category 6 — Identity Verification Follow-Up

| Inquiry Pattern | Approved Response | What NOT to Say |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "Why do you need my ID?" | "Identity verification is a standard part of our intake process. It confirms your identity for the safety of the clinical relationship and is required before your clinical evaluation can proceed." | Do not cite DEA or Ryan Haight Act to patients. Keep the explanation simple and non-alarming. |
| "My ID was declined, why?" | "I can look into that for you. Common reasons include image quality issues or document type. Let me check your file and I'll follow up with specific next steps." | Do not guess at the reason. Look up the actual review outcome and respond specifically. |
| "Can I use someone else's ID?" | "We require your own government-issued photo identification. We're unable to accept identification belonging to another person." | Do not make exceptions. |

## Category 7 — Urgent or Emergency Situations

| Inquiry Pattern | Approved Response | What NOT to Say |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Patient reports active chest pain, difficulty breathing, or other emergent symptoms | "If you're experiencing a medical emergency, please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency department immediately. We are a telehealth service and cannot provide emergency care." | Do not attempt to assess the emergency. Do not ask follow-up clinical questions. Direct to 911 immediately. |
| Patient reports suicidal ideation or intent to self-harm | "I hear you, and I want you to get help right now. Please call 988 (the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline) or go to your nearest emergency department. If you're in immediate danger, call 911." | Do not attempt to counsel. Do not continue the intake conversation. Prioritize the safety referral. |
| Patient reports adverse reaction to a medication | "If you're having a serious reaction, please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency department. If you're a current patient, please contact us through the patient portal so the Medical Director can follow up." | Do not provide medication-specific advice. Direct to emergency services for acute reactions. |

## Escalation Rule

If a pre-intake inquiry does not match any pattern above, or if the staff member is uncertain about the appropriate response:

1. Use the universal deferral: "That's a clinical question that our Medical Director will address after reviewing your complete intake. Would you like me to send you the intake link?"
2. Escalate to the MSO Owner within 1 business day
3. Document the inquiry pattern for potential addition to this script library in the next quarterly review

## Script Maintenance

This script library is reviewed quarterly by the MSO Owner. New inquiry patterns encountered in practice are added during the quarterly review. The quarterly review is documented in the SOP-002 revision log.
