## 1. Purpose

This SOP establishes the clinical procedures, governance framework, and documentation requirements for the synchronous video telehealth visit between the Covering Physician and a prospective Bloom Metabolics patient who has been approved under [SOP-003 (Clinical Eligibility Screening)](../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md).

The synchronous telehealth visit is the central clinical event in the Bloom Metabolics patient lifecycle. It is the encounter during which the Covering Physician performs the Good Faith Examination, delivers informed consent, evaluates the patient for treatment, and determines the prescribing pathway. Every downstream clinical action — prescribing, lab ordering, follow-up, monitoring — depends on the visit documented under this SOP.

This SOP specifically defines:

- Pre-visit preparation for the physician and the patient
- The Good Faith Examination as required by California Business & Professions Code section 2242 for telehealth encounters
- Service-line-specific informed consent delivery (TRT, GLP-1/GIP, Sexual Health, Longevity)
- The prescribing decision pathway: within-visit prescribing versus post-visit prescribing
- Visit documentation in OptiMantra as the medico-legal record of the encounter
- Handoff to future Prescribing SOPs for each service line
- Exception handling for technical failures, no-shows, mid-visit terminations, and acute emergencies

This SOP does NOT address:

- Dosing protocols, prescribing protocols, or medication selection (governed by future Prescribing SOPs per service line)
- Laboratory ordering or review (governed by future Lab Ordering & Review SOP)
- Refill or renewal visits (governed by future Refill & Renewal SOP)
- Injection education or self-administration training (governed by [SOP-004](../SOP-004-injection-education/SOP-004-injection-education.md))
- Patient intake (governed by [SOP-002](../SOP-002-patient-intake-operations/SOP-002-patient-intake-operations.md))
- Clinical eligibility screening (governed by [SOP-003](../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md))

## 3. Definitions

**Synchronous Video Telehealth Visit**
A real-time, audiovisual encounter between the Covering Physician and a patient conducted via a Bloom-Approved Telehealth Platform. The visit enables two-way interactive communication sufficient for the physician to perform a clinical evaluation. This is the modality by which Bloom Metabolics delivers clinical care.

**Good Faith Examination (GFE)**
The clinical evaluation a prescriber must perform prior to prescribing, as required by California Business & Professions Code section 2242. For telehealth encounters, the GFE must be conducted via real-time audiovisual communication and must include, at minimum, a patient history; a review of the patient's relevant medical records; and a physical examination, to the extent practicable via telehealth, sufficient to establish an informed diagnosis. The GFE is the legal predicate for any prescribing decision.

**Informed Consent (Service-Line-Specific)**
The process by which the Covering Physician ensures that the patient understands and voluntarily agrees to the proposed treatment, including its nature, expected benefits, material risks, alternatives (including no treatment), and any service-line-specific disclosures. Informed consent under this SOP is specific to the service line for which the patient was screened under [SOP-003](../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md) and is documented as an electronic acknowledgment in OptiMantra.

**Within-Visit Prescribing**
A prescribing pathway in which the Covering Physician makes the prescribing decision during the synchronous visit itself. This pathway is selected when the clinical picture is straightforward, laboratory results are acceptable, and no additional information is required. The prescription is submitted within 24 hours of the visit.

**Post-Visit Prescribing**
A prescribing pathway in which the Covering Physician defers the prescribing decision to a period following the visit. This pathway is selected when additional laboratory results are needed, the case is clinically complex, or the physician requires additional time for evaluation. The prescribing decision is communicated to the patient within 48 hours of the visit unless additional information is pending.

**Visit Note**
The contemporaneous medical record of the synchronous telehealth visit, documented in OptiMantra. The visit note is the medico-legal record of the encounter and must contain sufficient detail to reconstruct the clinical reasoning, findings, and decisions made during the visit. The visit note structure is defined in [Attachment C](attachments/attachment-C-visit-note-template.md).

**Audio-Only Fallback**
A contingency modality in which the visit is conducted via telephone audio when the video connection fails and cannot be restored. Audio-only fallback is permitted only under the conditions defined in section 7.2 of this SOP and is subject to the Covering Physician's clinical judgment regarding whether an adequate Good Faith Examination can be performed without video.

**Visit No-Show**
A scheduled visit at which the patient fails to appear within the grace period defined in section 7.3 of this SOP. A no-show triggers the rescheduling and disengagement workflow described therein.

**Mid-Visit Termination**
The discontinuation of a synchronous visit before the visit protocol is complete, initiated by either the Covering Physician or the patient. Grounds and documentation requirements for mid-visit termination are defined in sections 7.4 and 7.5 of this SOP.

**Technical Failure**
A disruption to the telehealth platform (video, audio, or both) that prevents the visit from being conducted or completed. Technical failure handling is defined in sections 7.2 and 7.7 of this SOP.

**Bloom-Approved Telehealth Platform**
A telehealth platform authorized for use at Bloom Metabolics that meets HIPAA security requirements and has a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) in place. The primary platform is OptiMantra (integrated EHR and telehealth). Approved alternatives for contingency use are Doxy.me and Zoom for Healthcare (each with executed BAA). No other platform may be used for clinical encounters without prior approval under [SOP-001](../SOP-001-document-control/SOP-001-document-control.md).

**Visit Identity Confirmation**
The real-time verification of the patient's identity at the start of the synchronous visit, performed by the Covering Physician. This confirmation supplements the identity verification performed during intake under [SOP-002](../SOP-002-patient-intake-operations/SOP-002-patient-intake-operations.md) and adopted during eligibility screening under [SOP-003](../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md). It ensures that the individual on the video call is the same individual whose records are under review.

**Patient-Stated Reason for Visit**
The patient's own description, in their own words, of why they are seeking care. The patient-stated reason for visit is documented in the visit note and compared against the service line for which they were screened under [SOP-003](../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md).

**Treatment Goals**
The patient's stated objectives for treatment, documented during the visit and incorporated into the treatment plan. Treatment goals inform the prescribing decision, monitoring cadence, and follow-up schedule.

**Service-Line-Specific Risks**
The material risks associated with the medications and treatments within a given service line. These risks are disclosed to the patient as part of the informed consent process. Service-line-specific risks are defined in Attachments D through G.

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Receipt from SOP-003

The synchronous telehealth visit workflow begins when a patient's GHL pipeline reaches the "Visit Scheduling" stage. This transition occurs when the Covering Physician documents a "Proceed" determination under [SOP-003 section 5.10](../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md).

**Entry conditions (all must be satisfied before a visit is scheduled):**

1. The patient has a documented "Proceed" determination in OptiMantra, signed by the Covering Physician under [SOP-003](../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md)
2. All operational gates from [SOP-002](../SOP-002-patient-intake-operations/SOP-002-patient-intake-operations.md) are confirmed (identity verification, California residency, payment, document acknowledgments)
3. The patient's clinical intake is complete in OptiMantra
4. Any pre-visit laboratory results required by SOP-003 for the patient's service line have been received and reviewed by the Covering Physician as part of the eligibility determination
5. The patient's GHL pipeline status is "Visit Scheduling"

If any entry condition is not met, the visit is not scheduled and the deficiency is routed back to the originating SOP for resolution.

### 5.2 Visit Scheduling

Upon confirmed entry into the "Visit Scheduling" pipeline stage, the visit is scheduled as follows:

1. **Scheduling method:** Visits are scheduled via OptiMantra's integrated scheduling module. The patient selects from available time slots published by the Covering Physician. If OptiMantra scheduling is not yet configured, the MSO Owner or MSO Intake Staff coordinates scheduling via direct communication (phone or secure message) and manually creates the OptiMantra appointment.

2. **Calendar integration:** All scheduled visits appear on the Covering Physician's OptiMantra calendar. Calendar notifications are sent to the Covering Physician and the patient per the platform's default notification settings.

3. **Visit duration:** `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Set the default visit duration for each service line. Industry-typical initial visit durations for reference: TRT 20-30 minutes, GLP-1/GIP 20-30 minutes, Sexual Health 15-20 minutes, Longevity 15-20 minutes. The physician should set durations based on their clinical workflow and the depth of evaluation required for each service line.]`

4. **Physician availability and scheduling cadence:** `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the physician's availability for telehealth visits — days of week, hours, maximum visits per day, and minimum interval between visits. This cadence determines the scheduling capacity of the practice and the patient wait time from eligibility approval to first visit.]`

5. **Scheduling window:** Visits are scheduled no sooner than 24 hours from the scheduling confirmation to allow time for pre-visit preparation per section 5.3. Visits are scheduled no later than 14 calendar days from the "Proceed" determination unless the patient requests a later date or scheduling constraints require it.

6. **Patient notifications:** Upon scheduling, the patient receives:
   - Confirmation of the visit date, time, and duration
   - Instructions for accessing the Bloom-Approved Telehealth Platform
   - The pre-visit preparation checklist per [Attachment A](attachments/attachment-A-pre-visit-preparation-checklist.md)
   - A reminder that government-issued photo identification must be available during the visit for identity confirmation

7. **Reminder cadence:** Automated reminders are sent at 48 hours and 1 hour before the scheduled visit via the patient's preferred communication channel (email, SMS, or OptiMantra portal notification).

### 5.3 Pre-Visit Preparation

Pre-visit preparation ensures that both the physician and the patient are ready for the encounter. The pre-visit preparation checklist is defined in [Attachment A](attachments/attachment-A-pre-visit-preparation-checklist.md).

**Patient preparation (communicated via Attachment A):**

1. Confirm access to a private, well-lit location with a stable internet connection
2. Test the telehealth platform link provided in the scheduling confirmation (recommend testing at least 1 hour before the visit)
3. Have government-issued photo identification available for identity confirmation
4. Have a current list of medications, supplements, and dosages available for review
5. Prepare questions about the proposed treatment, risks, and alternatives
6. Allow the full scheduled visit duration without interruption

**Physician preparation:**

1. Review the patient's OptiMantra record prior to the visit, including:
   - SOP-003 eligibility determination and clinical reasoning
   - Clinical intake (medical history, medications, allergies, current complaints)
   - Laboratory results on file
   - Service line of interest
   - Any flags or notes from the eligibility review
2. Minimum chart review time: `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Set the minimum time the physician requires for pre-visit chart review. This time is blocked on the physician's calendar before each visit. Typical: 5-10 minutes per patient.]`
3. Confirm that the service-line-specific informed consent document is ready for delivery (pre-loaded in OptiMantra or available for screen-share)
4. Confirm access to the Bloom-Approved Telehealth Platform and that audio/video equipment is functional

### 5.4 Visit Initiation and Identity Confirmation

The visit begins when the Covering Physician launches the telehealth session in the Bloom-Approved Telehealth Platform.

**Visit initiation:**

1. The Covering Physician opens the scheduled appointment in OptiMantra and initiates the video call
2. The Covering Physician confirms that audio and video are functioning for both parties
3. If the patient does not join within 5 minutes of the scheduled start time, the Covering Physician waits until the grace period defined in section 7.3 expires before marking the visit as a no-show

**Identity confirmation:**

Identity confirmation at the start of the visit is a clinical requirement that supplements the identity verification performed during intake under [SOP-002](../SOP-002-patient-intake-operations/SOP-002-patient-intake-operations.md) and adopted during eligibility screening under [SOP-003](../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md).

1. The Covering Physician asks the patient to hold their government-issued photo identification up to the camera
2. The Covering Physician visually compares the photo on the identification to the patient on camera
3. The Covering Physician verbally confirms the patient's full legal name as it appears on the identification
4. The Covering Physician verbally confirms the patient's date of birth
5. The Covering Physician verbally confirms that the patient is currently located in the State of California (California residency and physical presence during the encounter)
6. Additional identity confirmation steps: `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Specify any additional identity confirmation steps the physician requires beyond the standard protocol above. Examples to consider: confirmation of address on file, verification against a second identifier, specific questions to confirm identity in ambiguous cases.]`

**Identity confirmation failure:**

If the Covering Physician is unable to confirm the patient's identity — the photo does not match, the patient cannot produce valid identification, or the patient's verbal confirmations are inconsistent with records on file — the visit is terminated immediately. The physician documents the identity confirmation failure in OptiMantra, the GHL pipeline is updated to "Identity Confirmation Failed," and the matter is escalated per section 7.1 of this SOP.

**Documentation:** The Covering Physician documents in the visit note that identity was confirmed, the method used, and the identification type reviewed. If identity confirmation fails, the physician documents the basis for the failure.

### 5.5 Good Faith Examination

The Good Faith Examination is the clinical evaluation that satisfies the legal requirement of California Business & Professions Code section 2242 for telehealth prescribing. The GFE must be documented in the visit note and must demonstrate that the Covering Physician obtained sufficient clinical information to form an informed diagnosis and treatment recommendation.

The operational framework for the GFE is defined in [Attachment B](attachments/attachment-B-good-faith-examination-framework.md). The GFE includes, at minimum, the following elements across all service lines:

**Standard GFE elements (all service lines):**

1. **Patient-stated reason for visit:** The patient describes, in their own words, why they are seeking care. This is documented verbatim or in close paraphrase in the visit note.

2. **Review of medical history:** The Covering Physician reviews and confirms the medical history provided during clinical intake (OptiMantra), including:
   - Past medical history
   - Surgical history
   - Current medications and supplements (with dosages)
   - Known allergies and adverse reactions
   - Family history relevant to the service line
   - Social history (tobacco, alcohol, substance use, exercise, diet)

3. **Review of systems:** The Covering Physician conducts a review of systems relevant to the service line and the patient's presenting complaints.

4. **Review of laboratory results:** The Covering Physician reviews any laboratory results on file, confirms their relevance to the requested service line, and identifies any abnormalities or values requiring further evaluation.

5. **Telehealth physical examination:** To the extent practicable via synchronous video, the Covering Physician observes the patient's general appearance, affect, and any visible physical findings relevant to the service line. The physician documents what was observed and any limitations of the telehealth modality on the examination.

6. **Treatment goals:** The Covering Physician discusses and documents the patient's treatment goals.

7. **Assessment and clinical impression:** The Covering Physician formulates and documents a clinical impression based on the history, review of systems, laboratory review, and telehealth examination.

**Service-line-specific GFE elements:**

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the service-line-specific components of the Good Faith Examination for each service line. The standard elements above apply to all visits. The physician should specify the additional clinical inquiry, focused review of systems, and targeted telehealth examination components required for each service line. Examples for reference:

- **TRT:** Symptom assessment (fatigue, libido, mood, body composition, cognitive complaints); focused ROS (cardiovascular, urologic, psychiatric, endocrine); discussion of prior testosterone use; fertility status and goals; assessment of secondary sexual characteristics via video observation; confirmation that lab results meet diagnostic criteria for hypogonadism
- **GLP-1/GIP:** Weight history and prior weight loss attempts; dietary and exercise history; focused ROS (GI, endocrine, cardiovascular, psychiatric); assessment of BMI using stated height and weight; discussion of weight-related comorbidities; prior GLP-1 use and tolerability
- **Sexual Health:** Sexual function history; onset and duration of symptoms; focused ROS (cardiovascular, urologic, psychiatric, endocrine); discussion of relationship and psychosocial factors if clinically relevant; medication review with specific attention to nitrates and antihypertensives; most recent blood pressure reading
- **Longevity:** Health optimization goals; current supplementation regimen; focused ROS (energy, cognition, recovery, immune function); discussion of expectations and evidence base for requested therapies

The physician should specify the minimum depth of inquiry for each service line and the documentation standard for each element.]`

### 5.6 Service-Line-Specific Informed Consent

Following the Good Faith Examination, the Covering Physician delivers service-line-specific informed consent. Informed consent is a clinical and legal requirement. It must be obtained before any prescribing decision is made or communicated to the patient.

The informed consent content for each service line is defined in the following attachments:

- [Attachment D — TRT Informed Consent](attachments/attachment-D-trt-informed-consent.md)
- [Attachment E — GLP-1/GIP Informed Consent](attachments/attachment-E-glp1-informed-consent.md)
- [Attachment F — Sexual Health Informed Consent](attachments/attachment-F-sexual-health-informed-consent.md)
- [Attachment G — Longevity Informed Consent](attachments/attachment-G-longevity-informed-consent.md)

**Consent delivery method:**

1. The Covering Physician presents the informed consent document to the patient via one of the following methods:
   - Screen-share within the Bloom-Approved Telehealth Platform, reading through the consent with the patient in real time
   - Pre-sent to the patient via the OptiMantra patient portal prior to the visit, with verbal review during the visit
2. The Covering Physician reviews each section of the consent document with the patient, including:
   - Nature of the proposed treatment
   - Expected benefits
   - Material risks and potential side effects
   - Alternatives to the proposed treatment, including no treatment
   - Service-line-specific disclosures (fertility impact for TRT, GI risks for GLP-1/GIP, cardiovascular considerations for Sexual Health, evidence-base limitations for Longevity)
   - Monitoring requirements and the patient's commitment to follow-up
3. The Covering Physician provides the patient an opportunity to ask questions and addresses each question before proceeding
4. The patient electronically acknowledges the informed consent via OptiMantra (digital signature with timestamp)
5. If the patient declines to sign the informed consent, the visit proceeds to documentation (section 5.8) with the outcome recorded as "Declined by Patient — Consent Refused" and no prescribing decision is made

**Documentation:** The visit note records that informed consent was delivered, the method of delivery, the patient's questions (if any) and the physician's responses, and the timestamp of the patient's electronic acknowledgment. If consent was declined, the physician documents the patient's stated reason.

### 5.7 Prescribing Decision Pathway Selection

After the Good Faith Examination and informed consent, the Covering Physician selects one of two prescribing pathways. The prescribing pathway framework is defined in [Attachment H](attachments/attachment-H-prescribing-pathway-framework.md).

**Within-Visit Prescribing Pathway:**

This pathway is selected when the Covering Physician determines that the clinical picture supports a prescribing decision during the visit itself:

- The clinical presentation is consistent with the service-line indication
- Laboratory results are available and acceptable
- No additional clinical information is required
- The patient has acknowledged informed consent
- The physician is prepared to commit to a prescribing decision

Under the within-visit pathway, the Covering Physician:

1. Communicates the prescribing decision to the patient during the visit
2. Discusses the specific medication, formulation, and anticipated regimen (dosing details are governed by the applicable Prescribing SOP)
3. Discusses monitoring requirements and follow-up cadence
4. Documents the prescribing decision in the visit note
5. Submits the prescription within 24 hours of the visit via the applicable prescribing workflow

**Post-Visit Prescribing Pathway:**

This pathway is selected when the Covering Physician determines that additional evaluation is required before a prescribing decision can be made:

- Additional laboratory results are needed
- The case is clinically complex and requires further review
- Consultation with another provider is warranted
- The physician requires additional time to evaluate the risk-benefit profile

Under the post-visit pathway, the Covering Physician:

1. Communicates to the patient that the prescribing decision is deferred pending further evaluation
2. Specifies what additional information or evaluation is needed
3. Provides the patient a timeline for the prescribing decision (within 48 hours unless additional information is pending)
4. Documents the deferral, rationale, and pending items in the visit note
5. Communicates the prescribing decision to the patient upon completion of the additional evaluation via OptiMantra patient portal message, followed by phone outreach if the patient does not acknowledge within 24 hours

**Criteria for pathway selection:** `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the specific clinical criteria that determine when the within-visit pathway versus the post-visit pathway is appropriate for each service line. The operational framework above provides the structure; the physician should specify the clinical thresholds. Examples for reference:

- **Within-visit criteria:** All required labs within normal limits for the service line, no relative contraindications requiring additional evaluation, patient history consistent with service-line indication, patient has acknowledged consent, no outstanding clinical questions
- **Post-visit criteria:** Labs pending or outside expected range requiring confirmation, relative contraindications requiring documented risk-benefit analysis, patient reports symptoms suggesting a differential diagnosis not captured in eligibility screening, physician identifies need for additional specialist input

The physician should also specify whether within-visit prescribing is permitted for all service lines at launch or whether certain service lines default to the post-visit pathway (e.g., controlled substances may default to post-visit to allow for CURES check and additional review).]`

**Handoff to Prescribing SOPs:** Regardless of which pathway is selected, the prescribing decision itself and all downstream prescribing actions (medication selection, dosing, pharmacy routing, CURES reporting for controlled substances) are governed by the applicable future Prescribing SOP for the service line. This SOP governs the visit; the Prescribing SOP governs the prescription.

### 5.8 Visit Documentation

The visit note is the medico-legal record of the synchronous telehealth encounter. It must be completed in OptiMantra by the Covering Physician on the same day as the visit. The visit note template is defined in [Attachment C](attachments/attachment-C-visit-note-template.md).

**Required visit note elements:**

1. **Patient identifiers:** OptiMantra Patient ID, legal name, date of birth
2. **Visit metadata:** Date and time of visit, duration, platform used (OptiMantra, Doxy.me, or Zoom for Healthcare), service line
3. **Identity confirmation:** Method used, identification type reviewed, confirmation that identity was verified, California presence confirmed
4. **Reason for visit:** Patient-stated reason for visit, documented verbatim or in close paraphrase
5. **History of present illness:** Summary of the patient's current symptoms, complaints, and relevant history as discussed during the visit
6. **Medical history review:** Confirmation that medical history was reviewed and any updates or corrections noted during the visit
7. **Medications and allergies:** Current medication list as confirmed during the visit, with any changes from the intake record noted
8. **Review of systems:** Documented by system, with pertinent positives and negatives for the service line
9. **Telehealth examination findings:** Observations made via video, including general appearance, affect, and any visible findings; documentation of limitations of the telehealth modality
10. **Laboratory review:** Summary of laboratory results reviewed, with interpretation relevant to the service line
11. **Assessment and plan:** Clinical impression, diagnosis or working diagnosis, and treatment plan
12. **Informed consent:** Documentation that consent was delivered, method, patient questions and physician responses, electronic acknowledgment timestamp
13. **Prescribing pathway:** Within-visit or post-visit; rationale for pathway selection; if within-visit, the prescribing decision and medication prescribed; if post-visit, the pending items and expected timeline
14. **Treatment goals:** Patient's stated treatment objectives
15. **Follow-up:** Recommended follow-up cadence and next steps
16. **Visit outcome:** One of the outcomes defined in section 5.9
17. **Physician signature:** Electronic signature with date and time in OptiMantra

**Documentation standard:** The visit note must be sufficiently detailed that a peer reviewer, regulatory auditor, or medical board investigator could reconstruct the clinical reasoning, findings, and decisions made during the visit solely from the documentation. The note must demonstrate that a Good Faith Examination was performed, that informed consent was obtained, and that the prescribing decision (or deferral) was clinically supported.

### 5.9 Visit Outcome and Pipeline Update

At the conclusion of the visit, the Covering Physician assigns one of the following outcomes. The MSO Owner or MSO Intake Staff updates the GHL pipeline accordingly.

| Visit Outcome | Description | GHL Pipeline State |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Within-Visit Approved** | GFE complete, consent obtained, within-visit prescribing pathway selected, prescribing decision made | "Prescription Pending" (handoff to Prescribing SOP) |
| **Post-Visit Pending** | GFE complete, consent obtained, post-visit prescribing pathway selected, additional evaluation required | "Post-Visit Review" |
| **Declined by Physician** | GFE complete, physician determines patient is not appropriate for treatment based on visit findings | "Declined — Clinical (Visit)" |
| **Declined by Patient** | Patient declines treatment, declines informed consent, or withdraws during visit | "Declined — Patient (Visit)" |
| **Refer-Out** | Visit findings indicate need for specialist evaluation or care outside Bloom Metabolics' scope | "Referred Out (Visit)" |
| **Visit Failed — Technical** | Visit could not be completed due to technical failure per section 7.2 | "Visit Rescheduling" |
| **Visit Failed — No-Show** | Patient did not attend per section 7.3 | Per no-show protocol in section 7.3 |
| **Visit Failed — Terminated** | Visit terminated mid-encounter per section 7.4 or 7.5 | Per termination protocol in section 7.4 or 7.5 |

The visit outcome is documented in the visit note and in the GHL pipeline. The visit note is the authoritative record; the GHL pipeline state is the operational tracking mechanism.

### 5.10 Follow-Up Scheduling

For patients with outcomes of "Within-Visit Approved" or "Post-Visit Pending" who ultimately receive a prescription, follow-up visit scheduling is initiated per the monitoring cadence defined in the applicable Prescribing SOP.

**Service-line-specific follow-up cadences:** `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the follow-up visit cadence for each service line after the initial prescribing visit. Industry-typical cadences for reference:

- **TRT:** Follow-up visit at 6-8 weeks after initiation (with follow-up labs at 4-6 weeks), then every 3-6 months during the first year, then every 6-12 months if stable
- **GLP-1/GIP:** Follow-up visit at 4 weeks after initiation for dose titration assessment, then monthly during titration, then every 3 months once at maintenance dose
- **Sexual Health:** Follow-up as needed; initial check-in at 4 weeks; ongoing as clinically indicated
- **Longevity:** Follow-up at 4-8 weeks, then per physician determination based on treatment goals

The physician should specify the minimum follow-up cadences, what labs are required before each follow-up, and when a follow-up visit may be conducted asynchronously versus requiring a synchronous encounter.]`

**Handoff to Follow-Up Visit SOP:** Follow-up visits are governed by the future Follow-Up Visit SOP, which will define the follow-up encounter structure, monitoring assessments, treatment adjustment protocols, and re-consent requirements. Until the Follow-Up Visit SOP is drafted and approved, follow-up scheduling is managed per the Covering Physician's direct clinical instruction on a per-patient basis.

## 7. Escalation & Exception Handling

### 7.1 Identity Confirmation Failure

If the Covering Physician cannot confirm the patient's identity at the start of the visit per section 5.4:

1. The Covering Physician terminates the visit immediately and informs the patient that the visit cannot proceed without verified identity
2. The Covering Physician documents the identity confirmation failure in OptiMantra, including the specific basis for the failure (photo mismatch, no identification available, verbal inconsistencies, other)
3. The GHL pipeline is updated to "Identity Confirmation Failed"
4. The MSO Owner is notified of the failure within 1 business day
5. If the failure suggests potential identity fraud, the MSO Owner documents the incident and preserves all records for potential regulatory reporting
6. The patient may be offered one opportunity to reschedule if the failure was due to a correctable issue (e.g., identification not available at the time of the visit). The rescheduling communication is handled by the MSO Owner or MSO Intake Staff. If identity confirmation fails a second time, the patient's record is moved to "Declined — Identity" and no further visits are scheduled without escalation to the Covering Physician for a specific determination

### 7.2 Video Technical Failure

If the video connection fails during the visit and cannot be restored within a reasonable troubleshooting period:

1. The Covering Physician and patient attempt to restore the video connection using standard troubleshooting (refresh browser, rejoin call, switch to an approved alternative platform)
2. If video cannot be restored within 5 minutes of troubleshooting, the Covering Physician determines whether to proceed with audio-only or reschedule

**Audio-only fallback:** `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the conditions under which the Covering Physician may proceed with an audio-only visit when video fails. California law (Cal. B&P section 2290.5) permits synchronous audio-only telehealth under specified conditions. The physician should specify:

- Whether audio-only fallback is acceptable for initial visits (versus follow-up visits only)
- Whether audio-only fallback is acceptable for all service lines or only specific ones
- The minimum clinical elements that must have been completed via video before an audio-only fallback is acceptable (e.g., identity confirmation via video must be completed before audio-only fallback is permitted)
- Documentation requirements specific to audio-only visits (e.g., documentation that video was attempted, reason for fallback, clinical limitations noted)
- Whether an audio-only initial visit can support a within-visit prescribing decision or must default to post-visit prescribing]`

3. If the Covering Physician determines that an adequate Good Faith Examination cannot be performed without video, the visit is rescheduled. The visit note documents the technical failure, troubleshooting steps taken, and rationale for rescheduling.
4. The rescheduled visit is prioritized and scheduled within 7 calendar days of the failed visit at no additional cost to the patient.

### 7.3 Patient No-Show

If the patient does not join the scheduled visit:

1. **Grace period:** The Covering Physician waits 15 minutes past the scheduled start time. During this period, the MSO Owner or MSO Intake Staff attempts to reach the patient via phone and secure message to confirm whether the patient intends to join.
2. **No-show determination:** If the patient has not joined and has not communicated within the 15-minute grace period, the visit is marked as a no-show.
3. **Documentation:** The Covering Physician documents the no-show in OptiMantra: scheduled time, grace period observed, contact attempts made, patient did not appear.
4. **First no-show — rescheduling:** The GHL pipeline is updated to "Visit Rescheduling." The MSO Owner or MSO Intake Staff contacts the patient within 1 business day to reschedule. The patient is informed that a second no-show may result in disengagement from the scheduling queue.
5. **Second no-show — disengagement:** If the patient no-shows for a second scheduled visit, the GHL pipeline is updated to "Patient Disengaged — No-Show." No further visits are scheduled unless the patient contacts Bloom Metabolics to request reactivation, at which point the MSO Owner evaluates whether to re-enter the patient into the scheduling queue or require a new intake.

### 7.4 Mid-Visit Termination by Physician

The Covering Physician may terminate a visit before completing the full visit protocol for the following reasons:

1. **Clinical concern:** The physician identifies a clinical finding during the visit that requires immediate in-person medical evaluation (e.g., acute symptom presentation, safety concern). The physician directs the patient to seek appropriate in-person care and documents the clinical basis for termination.
2. **Patient behavior:** The patient is disruptive, threatening, intoxicated, or otherwise unable to participate in a clinical encounter. The physician informs the patient that the visit is being terminated and documents the behavior and rationale.
3. **Identity concern:** Post-initiation identity concerns arise during the visit (e.g., the patient's responses suggest they are not the individual whose records are under review). The physician terminates the visit and handles the matter per section 7.1.
4. **Informed consent refusal:** The patient refuses to acknowledge informed consent. The physician documents the refusal and terminates the clinical pathway. The visit is documented with outcome "Declined by Patient — Consent Refused."

**Documentation:** The Covering Physician documents the termination in the visit note, including: the point in the visit protocol at which termination occurred, the reason for termination, any clinical instructions given to the patient, and the disposition (rescheduled, declined, referred out, or disengaged).

**Pipeline update:** The GHL pipeline is updated based on the reason for termination: "Declined — Clinical (Visit)" for clinical concerns warranting decline, "Referred Out (Visit)" for referrals, "Patient Disengaged — Behavior" for behavioral terminations, or "Visit Rescheduling" if the physician determines that a rescheduled visit is appropriate.

### 7.5 Mid-Visit Termination by Patient

If the patient terminates the visit before completing the full visit protocol:

1. The Covering Physician documents the point at which the patient disconnected or stated they wished to end the visit
2. If the disconnection may be due to a technical failure rather than a voluntary termination, the MSO Owner or MSO Intake Staff attempts to contact the patient within 1 hour to determine the cause
3. If the patient voluntarily terminated: the visit outcome is "Declined by Patient (Visit)" and the GHL pipeline is updated to "Declined — Patient (Visit)"
4. If the disconnection was involuntary (technical failure): the visit is rescheduled per section 7.2
5. If the patient cannot be reached to clarify: the visit outcome is "Visit Failed — Terminated (Patient, Unconfirmed)" and the GHL pipeline is updated to "Visit Rescheduling." If the patient does not respond to outreach within 7 calendar days, the pipeline is updated to "Patient Disengaged — Lost Contact"

### 7.6 Acute Medical Emergency During Visit

If the patient reports or exhibits signs of an acute medical emergency during the visit:

1. The Covering Physician instructs the patient to call 911 immediately (or directs a person present with the patient to call 911)
2. The Covering Physician remains on the telehealth call, if possible, until emergency services are contacted
3. The Covering Physician does NOT attempt to manage the emergency via telehealth — the priority is connecting the patient with emergency services
4. The Covering Physician documents the emergency in OptiMantra: clinical presentation observed, instructions given, time 911 was contacted (if confirmed), time the telehealth call ended
5. The GHL pipeline is updated to "Emergency — Visit Terminated"
6. The MSO Owner is notified immediately
7. Follow-up: The MSO Owner or Covering Physician attempts to contact the patient within 24 hours to confirm their status and document the outcome
8. If the emergency involved a potential adverse event related to a Bloom Metabolics treatment (applicable only to follow-up visits, not initial visits), the Adverse Event SOP (future) governs reporting

### 7.7 Telehealth Platform Outage

If the primary telehealth platform (OptiMantra) is unavailable due to a system-wide outage:

1. The MSO Owner confirms the outage and notifies the Covering Physician
2. Visits scheduled during the outage are migrated to an approved alternative platform:
   - **First alternative:** Doxy.me (BAA executed)
   - **Second alternative:** Zoom for Healthcare (BAA executed)
3. The MSO Owner or MSO Intake Staff notifies affected patients of the platform change and provides the alternative access link
4. The Covering Physician conducts the visit on the alternative platform. All documentation requirements remain unchanged; the visit note is entered into OptiMantra once the platform is restored.
5. If no approved alternative platform is available, the visit is rescheduled within 7 calendar days at no additional cost to the patient
6. The MSO Owner documents the outage: date, time, duration, affected visits, alternative platform used, and resolution

**Competency demonstration:** Competency is demonstrated by reading the relevant sections, signing the acknowledgment in section 12, and completing a supervised walkthrough of the scheduling and pipeline update workflow before performing these tasks independently.
