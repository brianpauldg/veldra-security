# Attachment A — Pre-Visit Preparation Checklist

**Parent Document:** [SOP-005 — Synchronous Telehealth Visit](../SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit.md)

## Overview

This checklist is sent to patients 24--48 hours before a scheduled synchronous telehealth visit. It ensures the patient's environment, technology, and documentation are ready for a productive clinical encounter. MSO operations staff distribute this checklist via the automated appointment-reminder sequence in GoHighLevel.

This is an operational document prepared by the MSO. Clinical preparation items, where the Covering Physician wishes to add service-line-specific instructions, are marked accordingly.

---

## 1. Technical Setup

A stable, high-quality video connection is essential to a thorough telehealth examination. Please confirm each of the following before your visit.

- [ ] **Device:** Use a computer or tablet with a front-facing camera. A smartphone is acceptable but not preferred — larger screens allow the physician to conduct a more complete visual assessment.
- [ ] **Browser:** Use a current version of Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, or Apple Safari. Other browsers may not be fully supported by the telehealth platform.
- [ ] **Internet connection:** A minimum download and upload speed of 5 Mbps is required for reliable video. You can test your speed at [speedtest.net](https://www.speedtest.net). If your connection is unstable, consider using a wired Ethernet connection or positioning yourself closer to your Wi-Fi router.
- [ ] **OptiMantra portal login:** Log in to your OptiMantra patient portal at least once before the visit to confirm your credentials work. If you cannot access your portal, contact MSO support (see Section 9 below) before the day of your appointment.
- [ ] **Camera and microphone:** Test that your device's camera and microphone are functioning. Grant browser permissions for both when prompted. If using an external webcam or headset, verify those devices are selected in your system audio/video settings.

---

## 2. Privacy Environment

California telehealth regulations and sound clinical practice require that your visit take place in a setting that protects your privacy and allows for candid clinical discussion.

- [ ] **Private room:** You must be in a private, enclosed space for the duration of the visit.
- [ ] **Door closed:** Close and, if possible, lock the door to prevent interruptions.
- [ ] **Headphones recommended:** Wired or wireless headphones reduce the chance of others overhearing clinical discussion and improve audio quality for both parties.
- [ ] **No driving:** Do not join your visit while operating a vehicle. The physician will end the visit and ask you to reschedule.
- [ ] **No public spaces:** Coffee shops, waiting rooms, shared offices, and similar environments are not acceptable for a clinical telehealth encounter.
- [ ] **No others present during clinical discussion:** Other individuals should not be in the room during the clinical portion of the visit unless you have specifically requested their presence and the physician has agreed. If a support person will be present, inform the physician at the start of the encounter.

## 3. Documents to Have Ready

Having the following information accessible during your visit will allow the physician to conduct a thorough evaluation without unnecessary delays.

- [ ] **Current medication list:** Include all prescription medications, over-the-counter medications, and supplements. For each, note the name, dosage, and frequency.
- [ ] **Most recent lab results:** If you have had bloodwork within the past 90 days, have those results available — either printed or accessible on your screen. Labs older than 90 days may not be sufficient; the physician will advise if new labs are needed.
- [ ] **Prior medical records:** If you have records from previous providers that are relevant to your visit (e.g., prior hormone panels, imaging, specialist notes), have those accessible.
- [ ] **Government-issued photo ID:** Keep your ID nearby. The physician will confirm your identity at the start of the visit by comparing your appearance on video to your ID photograph.
- [ ] **List of questions for the physician:** Write down any questions or concerns you would like to address. Visits have a defined duration, and a prepared list helps ensure your priorities are covered.

### Good Faith Examination

During the visit, the physician will conduct what is called a Good Faith Examination. In plain terms, this is a clinical evaluation where the physician reviews your medical history, discusses your health goals, asks about your symptoms and current health status, and determines whether the treatment you are seeking is appropriate and safe for you. This is a legal and ethical requirement for telehealth prescribing in California and is identical in purpose to the evaluation you would receive in an in-person office visit.

### Informed Consent

The physician will review the informed consent for your specific treatment, including expected benefits, potential risks, alternative options, and what to expect during treatment. You will be asked to acknowledge and electronically sign the consent before any prescribing decision is made.

### Prescribing Decision

Depending on the findings of your evaluation, the physician may:

- Prescribe treatment during the same visit, if all clinical criteria are met.
- Require additional lab work or information before making a prescribing decision. In this case, the physician will explain what is needed and the timeline for follow-up.

A prescribing decision is always at the physician's sole clinical discretion.

### Follow-Up Scheduling

Before the visit concludes, the physician or MSO staff will discuss follow-up timing — whether for a lab review, dose adjustment, or routine re-evaluation.

## 5. Pre-Visit Video Test

We strongly recommend testing your video connection at least 24 hours before your appointment.

- [ ] Log in to the **OptiMantra patient portal**.
- [ ] Navigate to the video visit test page (linked in your appointment confirmation email).
- [ ] Confirm that you can see your own video feed and that your microphone registers audio.
- [ ] If the test fails, try a different browser or device, and contact MSO support if the issue persists.

---

## 6. Day-of-Visit Reminders

- [ ] Log in to the OptiMantra patient portal **5 minutes before** your scheduled appointment time.
- [ ] Confirm your camera and microphone are working.
- [ ] Have your medication list, lab results, ID, and questions within reach.
- [ ] Be in your private space with the door closed.
- [ ] Ensure your internet connection is stable — close unnecessary browser tabs and bandwidth-heavy applications.
- [ ] Silence your phone notifications to avoid interruptions.

---

## 7. If You Need to Reschedule

If you are unable to make your scheduled appointment:

- **Contact MSO support at least 24 hours before** your appointment time to reschedule at no charge.
- Rescheduling can be done via email, phone, or the OptiMantra patient portal.
- **No-show policy:** One reschedule is permitted without consequence. A second consecutive no-show (failure to appear without prior notice) will trigger a review of the patient's account status. The MSO will contact you to determine whether to rebook or close the intake.
