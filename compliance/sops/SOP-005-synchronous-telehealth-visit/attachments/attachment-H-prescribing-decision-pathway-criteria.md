# Attachment H — Prescribing Decision Pathway Criteria

**Parent Document:** [SOP-005 — Synchronous Telehealth Visit](../SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit.md)

---

## Overview

This attachment defines the decision criteria the Covering Physician uses to select between the within-visit prescribing pathway and the post-visit prescribing pathway for each patient encounter. The pathway choice is documented in the visit note (Attachment C) with the physician's rationale.

Both pathways are clinically valid. The distinction is operational timing, not clinical quality. The physician selects the pathway based on clinical readiness, not efficiency pressure.

---

## Within-Visit Prescribing Pathway

**Definition:** The physician makes the prescribing decision during the synchronous visit and documents the decision before the visit concludes.

**Timeline commitments:**
- Prescribing decision documented in OptiMantra: during or immediately after the visit
- Prescription sent to compounding pharmacy: within 24 hours of visit conclusion

**When to use (operational scaffolding — physician defines final criteria):**

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the criteria for within-visit prescribing. Suggested starting points for physician consideration:

- All required baseline labs have been reviewed and are within acceptable ranges
- No absolute or relative contraindications identified during the GFE
- The clinical case is straightforward with no complicating factors requiring additional review
- The patient demonstrates adequate understanding of the treatment, risks, and monitoring requirements
- The patient has acknowledged the service-line-specific informed consent
- The physician is confident in the prescribing decision without needing post-visit consultation or literature review

Service-line-specific considerations:
- TRT: Total testosterone confirmed low on two morning draws; hematocrit < [threshold]; PSA normal if applicable
- GLP-1/GIP: BMI meets threshold; no pancreatitis history; A1c reviewed; no MTC/MEN2 history
- Sexual Health: No nitrate use (for PDE5i); cardiovascular risk acceptable; no contraindications
- Longevity: Minimal clinical complexity; patient understands limited evidence base

## Post-Visit Prescribing Pathway

**Definition:** The physician defers the prescribing decision to a post-visit review period and documents the decision after completing additional evaluation.

**Timeline commitments:**
- Prescribing decision documented in OptiMantra: within 48 hours of visit conclusion
- Prescription sent to compounding pharmacy (if approved): within 24 hours of decision documentation
- Patient notified of decision: within 24 hours of decision documentation

**When to use (operational scaffolding — physician defines final criteria):**

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the criteria for post-visit prescribing. Suggested starting points:

- Additional labs are needed before a final prescribing decision can be made
- The clinical case is complex (multiple comorbidities, polypharmacy, borderline eligibility)
- The physician wants to consult clinical guidelines, peer literature, or a colleague before committing
- The patient has relative contraindications requiring documented risk-benefit analysis
- The patient requested time to consider the treatment decision
- Lab values are borderline and the physician wants to repeat before committing
- The visit was interrupted or shortened and clinical evaluation was not fully completed

- Sexual Health: [physician defines]
- Longevity: [physician defines]

Physician may add mandatory post-visit rules for specific clinical scenarios.]`

## Documentation Requirements

Regardless of pathway selected, the physician documents in the visit note:

1. **Pathway selected:** Within-visit or post-visit
2. **Rationale:** Why this pathway was chosen for this patient
3. **If within-visit:** Medication, dose, frequency, pharmacy, monitoring plan
4. **If post-visit:** What additional information is needed, timeline for decision, patient informed of timeline
5. **Patient communication:** Patient was informed of the pathway and timeline

## Patient Communication Templates

**Within-visit pathway (communicated during visit):**
"Based on my review of your history, labs, and our discussion today, I am ready to proceed with your treatment plan. I will send your prescription to the pharmacy within the next 24 hours. You will receive confirmation when it ships. Our first follow-up will be in [timeframe]."

**Post-visit pathway (communicated during visit):**
"I want to take some additional time to review [specific items] before finalizing your treatment plan. I will have a decision for you within 48 hours. You will hear from our team with the next steps. This is not a concern — it is part of my thorough review process."
