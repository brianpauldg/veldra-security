## Overview

This document provides the informed consent content for sexual health medications prescribed through Bloom Metabolics, including PDE5 inhibitors (Tadalafil, Sildenafil) and PT-141 (Bremelanotide). It satisfies the informed consent requirements under California Business and Professions Code Section 2290.5 (telehealth) and the Medical Board of California's guidelines on informed consent.

Where medications differ in risk profile, administration, or disclosures, medication-specific subsections are provided. All sections marked `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN]` require review, modification, and approval by the Covering Physician before this consent form is presented to patients.

## Consent Content

### 1. What Is Sexual Health Therapy?

Bloom Metabolics prescribes medications to address sexual dysfunction, including erectile dysfunction and hypoactive sexual desire. The medications available through this service line are:

- **Tadalafil** — a PDE5 inhibitor used for erectile dysfunction, available in daily or as-needed dosing.
- **Sildenafil** — a PDE5 inhibitor used for erectile dysfunction, taken as needed.
- **PT-141 (Bremelanotide)** — a melanocortin receptor agonist used for hypoactive sexual desire, administered via subcutaneous injection.

### 2. How Is It Administered?

**Tadalafil and Sildenafil** are taken orally.

- Tadalafil: available as daily low-dose or as-needed higher-dose formulation. As-needed dosing is taken prior to anticipated sexual activity.
- Sildenafil: taken approximately 30-60 minutes before anticipated sexual activity.

**PT-141 (Bremelanotide)** is administered via subcutaneous injection, typically at least 45 minutes before anticipated sexual activity.

### 10. Important Disclosures

1. **Absolute Nitrate Contraindication.** Patients prescribed PDE5 inhibitors (Tadalafil or Sildenafil) must never use nitrate medications in any form, including nitroglycerin, isosorbide mononitrate, isosorbide dinitrate, and recreational amyl nitrite ("poppers"). The combination can cause a severe, potentially fatal drop in blood pressure. If the patient is prescribed nitrates at any time during treatment, they must discontinue the PDE5 inhibitor immediately and notify their Bloom Metabolics physician.

2. **Not a Substitute for Cardiovascular Evaluation.** These medications treat symptoms of sexual dysfunction but do not address underlying cardiovascular or metabolic disease. Erectile dysfunction may be an early indicator of cardiovascular disease. The patient should discuss comprehensive cardiovascular risk assessment with their physician.

3. **PT-141 Hyperpigmentation.** Repeated use of PT-141 may cause darkening of the skin, particularly on the face and gums. This effect may not fully resolve after discontinuation.

4. **Telehealth Limitations.** This treatment is prescribed and managed via synchronous telehealth. Physical examination is limited to what can be assessed via audiovisual technology. The physician may require an in-person visit or referral to a local provider if clinical circumstances warrant.

5. **Medication-Specific Dosing.** Tadalafil (longer half-life, up to 36 hours) and Sildenafil (shorter half-life, 4-6 hours) differ in their duration of action and dosing approach. The patient should follow the specific instructions provided for their prescribed medication.
