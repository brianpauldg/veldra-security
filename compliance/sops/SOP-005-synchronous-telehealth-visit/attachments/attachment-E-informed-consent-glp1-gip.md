# Attachment E — Informed Consent: GLP-1/GIP Weight Management

**Parent Document:** [SOP-005 — Synchronous Telehealth Visit](../SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit.md)

---

## Overview

This document provides the informed consent content for GLP-1 and GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist therapy (Semaglutide and Tirzepatide) prescribed through Bloom Metabolics for weight management. It satisfies the informed consent requirements under California Business and Professions Code Section 2290.5 (telehealth) and the Medical Board of California's guidelines on informed consent.

Where Semaglutide and Tirzepatide differ in risk profile, administration, or disclosures, medication-specific subsections are provided. All sections marked `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN]` require review, modification, and approval by the Covering Physician before this consent form is presented to patients.

## Consent Content

### 1. What Is GLP-1/GIP Therapy?

GLP-1 receptor agonists (Semaglutide) and dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonists (Tirzepatide) are injectable medications used for chronic weight management. These medications work by mimicking naturally occurring hormones involved in appetite regulation, satiety signaling, and metabolic function.

Bloom Metabolics may prescribe either FDA-approved brand formulations or compounded versions of these medications. Patients will be informed which formulation they are receiving and the regulatory distinction between the two (see Section 10, Important Disclosures).

### 2. How Is It Administered?

Both Semaglutide and Tirzepatide are administered via subcutaneous injection, typically once weekly. Patients who elect self-administration will be trained in accordance with [SOP-004 Protocol 002 — GLP-1/GIP Subcutaneous Self-Administration](../../SOP-004-self-administration-protocols/attachments/protocol-002-glp1-gip-subcutaneous.md) prior to initiating home injection.

Both medications require a gradual dose titration schedule. The patient must follow the prescribed titration protocol; dose escalation without physician authorization is not permitted.

### 3. Expected Benefits

Patients commonly report meaningful weight reduction, improved appetite control, and improvements in metabolic markers during treatment. Individual results vary based on adherence, concurrent lifestyle modification, and physiological response.

### 10. Important Disclosures

1. **FDA-Approved vs. Compounded Formulations.** Bloom Metabolics may prescribe FDA-approved brand-name GLP-1/GIP medications or compounded versions prepared by a licensed 503A or 503B compounding pharmacy. Compounded medications are not FDA-approved and are prepared pursuant to a valid prescription to meet individual patient needs. The physician will disclose which formulation is prescribed, and the patient may ask questions about the distinction at any time.

2. **Pregnancy Contraindication.** GLP-1/GIP agonists are contraindicated in pregnancy. Patients of childbearing potential must use effective contraception during treatment and for at least 2 months following discontinuation, or as otherwise directed by the Covering Physician.

3. **Weight Regain After Discontinuation.** Clinical evidence indicates that weight regain is common following discontinuation of GLP-1/GIP therapy. Treatment may be indefinite to maintain results. The patient should discuss expectations for treatment duration with their physician.

4. **Cost and Insurance.** GLP-1/GIP medications prescribed for weight management through Bloom Metabolics are cash-pay and are not typically covered by insurance. The patient is responsible for ongoing medication costs. Pricing may change based on formulation, dose, and market availability.

5. **Dose Titration Compliance.** The patient must follow the prescribed dose titration schedule. Escalating the dose without physician authorization may increase the risk and severity of side effects.

6. **Self-Administration Responsibility.** Patients who self-administer injections accept responsibility for following the self-administration protocol as trained. Refer to [SOP-004 Protocol 002](../../SOP-004-self-administration-protocols/attachments/protocol-002-glp1-gip-subcutaneous.md) for technique, sharps disposal, and adverse event reporting procedures.

7. **Telehealth Limitations.** This treatment is prescribed and managed via synchronous telehealth. Physical examination is limited to what can be assessed via audiovisual technology. The physician may require an in-person visit or referral to a local provider if clinical circumstances warrant.
