## Consent Content

### 1. What Are NAD+ and Glutathione Therapies?

**NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide)** is a coenzyme present in all living cells that plays a central role in cellular energy metabolism, DNA repair, and gene expression regulation. NAD+ levels decline with age. Supplemental NAD+ is provided as an optimization therapy intended to support cellular function.

**Glutathione** is an endogenous antioxidant produced by the body that plays a role in detoxification, immune function, and protection against oxidative stress. Supplemental glutathione is provided as an optimization therapy intended to support antioxidant capacity.

Both are typically provided as compounded products. They are not FDA-approved for specific indications.

### 10. Important Disclosures

1. **Limited Evidence Base.** NAD+ and glutathione optimization therapies have emerging but limited clinical evidence supporting their use for the indications discussed. Much of the existing evidence is preclinical (animal or cell-based studies) or derived from small-scale human studies. The patient should understand that these therapies are not established standard-of-care treatments.

2. **FDA Status.** NAD+ and glutathione provided through Bloom Metabolics are compounded products prepared by a licensed 503A or 503B compounding pharmacy. They are not FDA-approved for specific indications. Compounded products do not undergo the same regulatory review as FDA-approved drugs.

3. **Optimization, Not Treatment of Disease.** These therapies are offered as wellness and optimization interventions. They are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Patients with diagnosed medical conditions should continue to receive treatment from their primary care physician or specialist.

4. **No Guaranteed Outcomes.** Individual results vary. Some patients report subjective improvements; others do not. There is no guarantee of any specific outcome.

5. **Cost.** These are elective optimization therapies and are not covered by insurance. The patient is responsible for all costs. Pricing may vary based on formulation, administration route, and dose.

6. **Telehealth Limitations.** This treatment is prescribed and managed via synchronous telehealth. Physical examination is limited to what can be assessed via audiovisual technology. The physician may require an in-person visit or referral to a local provider if clinical circumstances warrant.
