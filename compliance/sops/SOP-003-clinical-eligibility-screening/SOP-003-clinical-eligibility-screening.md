## 1. Purpose

This SOP establishes the clinical eligibility criteria, contraindication review process, and risk stratification framework by which the Covering Physician determines whether a prospective Bloom Metabolics patient may proceed to a clinical telehealth visit, must be declined, or should be referred elsewhere.

This SOP is the clinical complement to [SOP-002 (Patient Intake Operations)](../SOP-002-patient-intake-operations/SOP-002-patient-intake-operations.md). SOP-002 operates the operational gates (identity verification, California residency, payment, document acknowledgment); SOP-003 sets the clinical criteria those gates enforce and performs the clinical eligibility review that follows.

This SOP specifically addresses eligibility determination — the decision of whether a patient is appropriate for the requested service line. It does not address dosing, prescribing, or treatment plan formulation, which are governed by future Prescribing SOPs.

## 3. Definitions

**Clinical Eligibility**
The determination by the Covering Physician that a prospective patient meets the clinical criteria to proceed to a telehealth visit for a specific service line. Clinical eligibility is distinct from operational eligibility (confirmed by SOP-002) and from the treatment decision itself (made during the telehealth visit).

**Inclusion Criteria**
The clinical characteristics a patient must demonstrate to be eligible for a given service line. Inclusion criteria are set by the Covering Physician and documented in §5.3 through §5.6 of this SOP.

**Exclusion Criteria**
Clinical characteristics that render a patient ineligible for a given service line. Exclusion criteria include both absolute and relative contraindications.

**Absolute Contraindication**
A clinical condition, finding, or circumstance that categorically precludes a patient from receiving treatment in a given service line. The presence of an absolute contraindication results in a Decline decision.

**Relative Contraindication**
A clinical condition, finding, or circumstance that increases risk but does not categorically preclude treatment. Relative contraindications require documented clinical reasoning by the Covering Physician if the decision is to proceed. The Covering Physician may proceed with heightened monitoring, modified protocols, or additional informed consent.

**Risk Stratification**
The clinical assessment process by which the Covering Physician categorizes a prospective patient as: Proceed (eligible for clinical visit), Decline (ineligible due to absolute contraindication or unacceptable risk), or Refer Out (condition outside Bloom Metabolics' scope requiring evaluation by another provider).

**Proceed-to-Visit Decision**
The Covering Physician's documented determination that a patient is clinically eligible to advance to a telehealth visit for the requested service line.

**Decline Decision**
The Covering Physician's documented determination that a patient is clinically ineligible for the requested service line due to contraindications or unacceptable risk. A Decline may be service-line-specific (patient may be eligible for a different service line) or universal (patient is ineligible for all Bloom Metabolics services).

**Refer-Out Decision**
The Covering Physician's documented determination that a patient's condition requires evaluation or treatment by a provider outside Bloom Metabolics' scope. A referral is not a decline — it is a clinical routing decision indicating that the patient may benefit from care that Bloom Metabolics is not equipped to provide.

**Service Line**
A category of clinical services offered by Bloom Metabolics. Service lines at launch: TRT, GLP-1/GIP Weight Management, Sexual Health, and Longevity. Each service line has its own eligibility criteria defined in §5.3 through §5.6.

**Pre-Visit Lab Review**
The Covering Physician's review of laboratory results submitted during intake or ordered prior to the first clinical visit. Certain laboratory baselines are required before the Covering Physician will schedule a clinical visit. Required labs are defined per service line in §5.3 through §5.6.

**Good Faith Examination**
The evaluation a prescriber must conduct before prescribing, as required by California Bus. & Prof. Code §2242 for telehealth encounters. The Good Faith Examination is performed during the clinical telehealth visit (governed by future SOP), not during eligibility screening. Eligibility screening determines whether the patient advances to the visit where the Good Faith Examination occurs.

**Standard of Care**
The degree of care, skill, and treatment that a reasonably competent and skilled healthcare professional, with a similar background and in the same medical community, would have provided under the circumstances. Clinical eligibility criteria in this SOP are set by the Covering Physician to be consistent with the applicable standard of care.

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 Receipt of Handoff Payload from SOP-002

When a prospective patient's GHL pipeline reaches "Ready for Clinical Review," the Covering Physician receives a structured handoff payload as specified in [SOP-002 Attachment E — Handoff Payload Specification](../SOP-002-patient-intake-operations/attachments/attachment-E-handoff-payload-specification.md).

**Payload contents (confirmed by SOP-002):**

- Patient identifier (OptiMantra Patient ID)
- Verified legal name and contact information
- Service line of interest
- Identity verification outcome: Verified, with reviewer name, date, and affirmation statement
- California residency confirmation method
- Date of birth, age, and confirmation that age meets the service-line minimum defined in this SOP
- Reference to completed OptiMantra clinical intake (the Covering Physician accesses the clinical content directly in OptiMantra)
- Acknowledgment confirmations with timestamps: NPP, General Consent for Care, HIPAA Authorization
- Corepay transaction reference ID and payment date

**Physician review of payload:**

1. The Covering Physician opens the patient's OptiMantra record
2. The Covering Physician reviews the handoff summary confirming all operational gates are passed
3. The Covering Physician confirms identity verification status — this confirmation is the clinical adoption of the operational identity verification performed under [SOP-002 §5.8](../SOP-002-patient-intake-operations/SOP-002-patient-intake-operations.md) and forms part of the basis for the subsequent Good Faith Examination
4. The Covering Physician proceeds to review the clinical intake content (medical history, medications, allergies, labs, reason for treatment)

### 5.9 Decline and Refer-Out Communication

When the Covering Physician makes a Decline or Refer Out determination, the patient is notified as follows:

1. The Covering Physician documents the decision and approved communication in OptiMantra per §5.8
2. MSO staff (or the MSO Owner) delivers the patient-facing communication using the approved language from [Attachment B — Decline and Refer-Out Language](attachments/attachment-B-decline-and-refer-language.md)
3. Communication is delivered via:
   - OptiMantra patient portal message (primary channel)
   - Phone outreach by MSO staff using approved scripts (secondary channel, within 2 business days)
4. The communication is documented in OptiMantra: date, method, staff member, language used
5. The GHL pipeline is updated to "Declined — Clinical" or "Referred Out" based on the physician's determination

**Communication boundary:** The patient-facing decline or refer-out communication uses the approved language in Attachment B. MSO staff do not improvise clinical explanations or engage in clinical discussion with the patient about the decline. If the patient has clinical questions about the decline, the MSO Owner escalates to the Covering Physician for a direct physician-patient communication if clinically appropriate.

### 5.10 Handoff to Telehealth Visit (Governed by Future SOP)

When the Covering Physician documents a "Proceed" decision:

1. The determination is documented in OptiMantra per §5.8
2. The GHL pipeline transitions from "Ready for Clinical Review" to "Visit Scheduling"
3. "Visit Scheduling" is the entry state of the future Synchronous Telehealth Visit SOP
4. The patient is notified that they are approved for a clinical visit and provided scheduling instructions

The future Telehealth Visit SOP will define: visit scheduling workflow, pre-visit preparation, the synchronous telehealth encounter, the Good Faith Examination, prescribing decision, and post-visit documentation. Until the Telehealth Visit SOP is drafted and approved, the "Visit Scheduling" pipeline stage is a placeholder and no visits are conducted.

## 7. Escalation & Exception Handling

### 7.1 Incomplete Clinical Intake

If the clinical intake in OptiMantra is incomplete (missing sections, unanswered questions, insufficient detail):

1. The Covering Physician documents the deficiency in OptiMantra
2. MSO staff contacts the patient requesting completion of the missing information
3. The patient record remains in "Ready for Clinical Review" until the intake is complete
4. If the patient does not respond within 14 calendar days, the record is moved to "Abandoned — Incomplete Clinical Intake"

### 7.2 Borderline Eligibility

If a patient's eligibility is borderline (e.g., BMI slightly below threshold, lab values near exclusion range, clinical history ambiguous):

1. The Covering Physician documents the borderline finding and clinical reasoning
2. The Covering Physician may: (a) request additional information or labs from the patient, (b) proceed with heightened monitoring and documented rationale, or (c) decline with clear documentation
3. Borderline determinations are never delegated to MSO staff

### 7.3 Patient Disputes Clinical Decision

If a patient disputes a Decline or Refer Out determination:

1. MSO staff acknowledges the patient's concern and escalates to the MSO Owner
2. The MSO Owner evaluates whether the concern raises new clinical information not previously reviewed
3. If new clinical information is presented, the MSO Owner routes it to the Covering Physician for reconsideration
4. The Covering Physician reviews and may affirm, modify, or reverse the original determination
5. The reconsideration and outcome are documented in OptiMantra
6. If the original determination is affirmed, the patient is informed that the clinical decision is final and they may seek care from another provider

### 7.4 Multiple Service Line Requests

If a patient requests eligibility screening for multiple service lines:

1. The Covering Physician evaluates eligibility for each requested service line independently
2. A patient may be eligible for one service line and declined for another
3. Each service line determination is documented separately in OptiMantra
4. The patient is informed of their eligibility status for each requested service line
