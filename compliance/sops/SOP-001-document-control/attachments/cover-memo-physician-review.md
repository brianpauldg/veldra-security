## Why You Are Receiving This

Welcome to Bloom Metabolics. This memo accompanies the first Standard Operating Procedure I am asking you to review and sign as our covering physician (Medical Director). It is also the first communication in what I expect will be an ongoing review cadence for our clinical SOP system over the coming months.

Bloom Metabolics is a California cash-pay telehealth clinic providing hormone optimization (TRT), GLP-1/GIP weight management, sexual health, and (post-LegitScript certification) peptide therapy. The clinic operates under a Management Services Organization (MSO) and Professional Corporation (PC) structure as required by California's corporate practice of medicine doctrine. I am the MSO Owner. The PC, which holds the clinical license to practice medicine and which you serve as Medical Director under the Doctors for Providers retainer arrangement, employs or contracts you for that role.

This memo and the attached SOP are administrative documents issued by the MSO on behalf of the PC's clinical governance function. The MSO is requesting your clinical review and signature in your capacity as the PC's Medical Director.

## What SOP-001 Is

SOP-001 is the foundational document control instrument for the Bloom Metabolics SOP system. It defines how all subsequent clinical and operational SOPs will be created, reviewed, approved, distributed, revised, and retired. It is not itself a clinical protocol — it is the governance framework under which clinical protocols (TRT prescribing, GLP-1 management, adverse event handling, etc.) will be developed and brought to you for clinical approval over the coming months.

I am asking you to review and sign SOP-001 first because every clinical SOP that follows will inherit its governance, version control, and signature workflow from this document. Establishing the framework before generating clinical content protects both of us: it ensures every clinical SOP you sign has clear provenance, clear scope, a documented review trail, and a defined revision pathway.

## What I Am Asking You to Review

Your review of SOP-001 is focused on three specific questions:

1. **Does the governance framework adequately protect clinical practice from inappropriate MSO direction?** Section 2.3 (Corporate Structure Acknowledgment) and the MSO non-interference principle articulate that clinical content authority rests with you. Section 4 (Roles & Responsibilities) splits the Owner role into MSO and PC variants reflecting this. Please review whether these provisions are clinically sufficient and whether you would propose any additional safeguards.

2. **Are the review and approval workflows clinically operable?** Sections 5.4 through 5.7 describe how clinical SOPs will be routed to you for review, what the review must address (Section 5.5), how feedback is captured, and how signatures are obtained. Please review whether the proposed workflow is realistic given your other clinical commitments and the Doctors for Providers retainer terms. Specific items to consider: the DOCX-based redline process, the proposed signature method (electronic vs. wet — Section 5.7 defaults foundational documents to wet signature), and the proposed turnaround expectations.

3. **Are the escalation pathways clinically appropriate?** Section 7.3 defines a Critical tier for SOP-regulation conflicts that create immediate patient safety risk, with a 4-hour physician contact window. Section 5.10 lists triggers that accelerate SOP review (regulatory change, adverse events, guideline changes, etc.). Please review whether these timelines are realistic for you to meet under retainer and whether the trigger list is complete.

I am not asking you to review SOP-001 for substantive clinical content, because there is no substantive clinical content in this document — clinical content lives in the SOPs that will follow. SOP-001 is the framework; SOPs 002 onward are the clinical instruments.

## What I Am NOT Asking You to Review

To respect your time and the boundaries of your role, please note the following items are explicitly NOT within the scope of your review:

- **Corporate structure validity:** The MSO/PC arrangement and its compliance with California corporate practice of medicine doctrine is the responsibility of corporate counsel. The §2.3 description is informational so you understand the framework you are operating within; it is not for you to ratify.
- **Specific California regulatory citations:** Section 9 cites California statutes (CMIA, the Information Practices Act, B&P §2400 et seq., B&P §2242, B&P §2220.05). These citations are subject to separate review by California-competent legal counsel. If you spot something obviously incorrect, flag it; otherwise, treat them as pending counsel review.
- **HIPAA, DEA, or FDA regulatory specifics:** Same posture as above. The references are scaffolding; specialized counsel will validate them. Your review focuses on clinical operability.
- **The 19 future SOPs listed in the Master Index:** Those are upcoming work. SOP-001 governs how they will be developed; you are not approving their content by approving SOP-001.
- **Operational SOPs (marketing, IT, vendor management):** These are MSO documents and do not require your approval per Section 2.3.

## What Happens After Your Review

Three pathways are possible after your review:

1. **You approve as-is:** You sign the signature page on SOP-001 v0.3 and return the signed document. SOP-001 transitions from Draft to Approved, and I begin scheduling the legal counsel review for the items flagged in the v0.3 revision history (corporate practice framing, e-signature legal references, California privacy citations, Provisional status duration).

2. **You request revisions:** You return SOP-001 with redlines or a written review memo identifying the items you want changed. I produce a v0.4 incorporating your feedback, and we run a second review cycle.

3. **You decline to approve in current form:** If there is something structural that prevents your approval, we have a synchronous conversation (phone or video) to identify the path forward.

## Format for Your Response

Please return your review in one of the following formats, in order of my preference:

1. **Redlined DOCX:** Track changes turned on in Microsoft Word or equivalent, with comments on substantive items. This is the cleanest audit trail and is the format the SOP system is designed around.
2. **Written review memo:** A separate document identifying the items you want changed, organized by SOP section. Acceptable if redlining is impractical.
3. **Email or phone conversation:** Acceptable for clarifying questions, but any substantive feedback should be captured in writing per the SOP's own requirements.
