## 1. Welcome and Purpose of This Memo

Thank you for joining Bloom Metabolics as Covering Physician. This memo establishes the scope, sequence, and target turnaround for your initial review and approval of the Bloom Metabolics Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) system.

Bloom Metabolics is a California cash-pay telehealth clinic in pre-operational stage. Service lines at launch include Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT), GLP-1/GIP weight management (semaglutide and tirzepatide), sexual health (PT-141, tadalafil, sildenafil), and longevity (NAD+, glutathione). Peptide therapy (BPC-157, TB-500) is deferred pending LegitScript Healthcare Merchant Certification and compounding pharmacy 503A licensure.

The clinic operates under a Management Services Organization (MSO) and Professional Corporation (PC) structure, with the PC currently in formation under the ProMed friendly-PC / stock-transfer arrangement. You are retained as Covering Physician via Doctors for Providers on a monthly retainer arrangement.

The clinic has not yet treated any patients. Seven SOPs have been drafted in advance of operational launch precisely so the SOP system, your clinical workflows, and the corporate structure can all be reviewed together before patient care begins. This memo asks for your engagement in that review.

## 2. Scope of Your Review

Your review covers the clinical content of seven SOPs currently in Draft status. Each SOP is governed by SOP-001 (Document Control), which establishes the lifecycle, versioning, and approval framework for all SOPs in the system.

The MSO Owner has prepared each SOP as operational scaffolding under the operational delegation provision of SOP-001 §2.3. This means structural sections (Purpose, Scope, Definitions, Roles, Documentation, Compliance References, Related Documents) are drafted and ready for your review. Clinical sections contain `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN]` markers wherever your clinical input, judgment, or approval is required.

**You are not expected to validate corporate structure, regulatory citations, or non-clinical operational mechanics.** Those areas are reviewed separately by the MSO Owner and California-competent legal counsel. Your review is focused on:

- Clinical content adequacy — are the clinical parameters, criteria, and decision rules consistent with current standard of care?
- Workflow operability — can you, as Covering Physician, execute the procedures defined in these SOPs in your daily clinical work?
- Escalation appropriateness — do the documented escalation pathways align with your clinical judgment and California Medical Board expectations?
- Documentation defensibility — would the documentation generated under these SOPs be defensible under audit, malpractice review, or California Medical Board scrutiny?

A note on the scope: this is a substantive engagement. Total realistic review time across all seven SOPs is approximately 13 hours of structured work. We have sized the review intentionally — a thorough first-physician onboarding done before patient care begins is materially more efficient than building the SOP system reactively after the clinic is operating. We compensate beyond the standard DfP retainer where the engagement scope warrants it; please discuss with us if your fee structure requires adjustment.

## 3. Current SOP Queue and Your Required Actions

Seven SOPs are in current queue. Each is briefly described below with what is required from you.

### 3.1 SOP-001 (Document Control & SOP Lifecycle Management) — v0.3, Draft

**What it does:** Establishes how every SOP in the Bloom Metabolics system is created, versioned, reviewed, approved, retired, and audited. It is the meta-SOP that governs the system.

**Your role:** Review for governance framework adequacy. Your specific input is requested on:
- Whether the Provisional status mechanism (§5.6, 180-day clock with 90-day milestone) is structurally defensible
- Whether the change request workflow (§5.13) is executable in practice for clinical SOP modifications
- Whether the accelerated review triggers (§5.10) capture the right operational events
- The §2.3 MSO/PC ownership boundary and whether the operational delegation provision adequately supports your clinical authority

**Estimated review time:** 1.5 hours
**Target turnaround:** 14 calendar days from receipt

### 3.2 SOP-002 (Patient Intake Operations) — v0.2, Draft, MSO-Owner-Ready

**What it does:** Governs the operational intake workflow from website inquiry through to handoff for clinical eligibility screening. Includes data classification rules (GHL marketing-only versus OptiMantra clinical), identity verification, payment processing, and document delivery.

**Your role:** This is an operational SOP under MSO ownership. The MSO Owner has signing authority. **You are not asked to approve this SOP.** Review is for awareness only — you should understand what operational gates a patient passes through before reaching your clinical workflow at SOP-003.

**Estimated review time:** 30 minutes
**Target turnaround:** No deadline; review at your convenience

### 3.3 SOP-003 (Clinical Eligibility Screening) — v0.1, Draft Scaffold

**What it does:** Defines the clinical eligibility criteria, contraindication review, and risk stratification framework by which you determine whether a prospective patient may proceed to a clinical telehealth visit, must be declined, or should be referred elsewhere. Service-line-specific eligibility for TRT, GLP-1/GIP, Sexual Health, and Longevity.

**Your role:** This is a clinical SOP under your ownership. You are Document Owner and Approving Authority. Required clinical input includes:
- Service-line-specific age minimums (TRT, GLP-1, Sexual Health, Longevity)
- Required pre-visit laboratory panels per service line (cross-references SOP-006)
- Absolute and relative contraindications per service line
- Decline criteria and refer-out criteria
- Risk stratification thresholds

**Estimated review time:** 2 hours
**Target turnaround:** 14 calendar days from receipt

### 3.4 SOP-004 (Patient Self-Administration Injection Education) — v0.1, Draft Scaffold

**What it does:** Governs patient-facing self-administration injection education materials including printable inserts, downloadable handouts, and web pages routed via QR codes on medication packaging. Five protocols at full scope: TRT IM, GLP-1/GIP SubQ, HCG SubQ, BPC-157 SubQ (future), TB-500 SubQ (future).

**Your role:** Clinical SOP under your ownership. The MSO Owner has prepared protocol scaffolding using current evidence-based parameters (gauges, angles, anatomical sites, rotation cadence) consistent with FDA prescribing information and established clinical guidelines. Your review and approval is required to transition the SOP from Draft to Provisional under SOP-001 §5.6. Required clinical input includes:
- Validation or modification of injection technique parameters per protocol
- Site rotation specifics
- Aspiration guidance
- Patient communication thresholds (when patients should call the care team)
- Any service-line-specific clinical considerations not captured in scaffolding

**Estimated review time:** 1.5 hours
**Target turnaround:** 14 calendar days from receipt

### 3.5 SOP-005 (Synchronous Telehealth Visit) — v0.1, Draft Scaffold

**What it does:** Governs the synchronous video telehealth visit between you and a prospective patient. Includes pre-visit preparation, identity confirmation, the Good Faith Examination per California Bus. & Prof. Code §2242, service-line-specific informed consent, prescribing pathway selection (within-visit vs. post-visit), visit documentation, and exception handling.

**Your role:** Clinical SOP under your ownership. Significant clinical input required given that this SOP governs the central clinical encounter. Required clinical input includes:
- Visit duration per service line
- Service-line-specific Good Faith Examination elements
- Service-line-specific informed consent content (TRT, GLP-1/GIP, Sexual Health, Longevity)
- Within-visit vs. post-visit prescribing decision criteria
- Audio-only fallback acceptable circumstances per California §2290.5
- Service-line-specific follow-up cadences
- Your availability windows and visit scheduling capacity

**Estimated review time:** 3 hours
**Target turnaround:** 21 calendar days from receipt (longer due to scope)

### 3.6 SOP-006 (Lab Ordering & Review) — v0.2, Draft, Hybrid

**What it does:** Hybrid clinical/operational SOP governing laboratory ordering, results delivery, results review, and abnormal results escalation across all service lines. LabCorp is the sole lab vendor at launch; lab costs are bundled into the patient's service tier price.

**Your role:** Hybrid ownership per SOP-001 §2.3. Operational components have been signed by the MSO Owner. Clinical components require your approval. Required clinical input includes:
- Pre-visit lab panels per service line (Attachment A)
- Post-visit monitoring lab cadence per service line (Attachment B)
- Results review SLAs (§5.4 — currently proposed: Critical 4-hour acknowledgment, Abnormal Actionable 1 business day, Abnormal Non-Actionable 3 business days, Normal 3 business days)
- Per-service-line policy on whether pre-visit labs are mandatory
- Approval of clinical-adjacent language in patient communication templates (Attachment E)

**Estimated review time:** 1.5 hours
**Target turnaround:** 14 calendar days from receipt

### 3.7 SOP-007 (Prescribing Protocols) — v0.2, Draft

**What it does:** Meta-SOP governing all prescribing activity at Bloom Metabolics, with service-line-specific clinical content housed in attachments. Establishes the three-phase treatment model (Initiation, Titration, Maintenance), the hybrid pharmacy architecture (Strive Pharmacy for FDA-approved testosterone cypionate; 503A/503B compounding partner for compounded products), Schedule III controlled substance prescribing protocols including DEA verification and CURES check requirements, the async refill pathway for non-controlled medications, the modified-async refill pathway for controlled substances, and the compounded GLP-1 exception pathway with conservative default-branded posture.

**Your role:** Clinical SOP under your ownership. This is the largest and most clinically substantive SOP in the queue, and it carries the most regulatory weight because it governs Schedule III controlled substance prescribing. Required clinical input includes:
- Attachment A — TRT Prescribing Protocol: starting doses, titration logic, target ranges (total T, free T, hematocrit, estradiol), Schedule III-specific dose-stability criteria, treatment hold/discontinuation criteria
- Attachment B — GLP-1/GIP Prescribing Protocol: default product preferences (semaglutide vs tirzepatide), titration approach, compounded GLP-1 medical-necessity criteria including the §5.9 (4) catch-all that we explicitly deferred to your authority
- Attachment C — Sexual Health Prescribing Protocol: tadalafil, sildenafil, PT-141 starting doses and dose-adjustment logic
- Attachment D — Longevity Prescribing Protocol: NAD+ and glutathione protocols (deferred pending LegitScript and partner finalization)
- §5.4 CURES concerning-finding decision tree and Schedule III refill authorization preferences
- §5.5 async refill pathway eligibility thresholds per service line
- Confirmation that your California DEA registration is active and unrestricted

**Critical pre-engagement items:** Before signing, please confirm that your California DEA registration is active and unrestricted, and that you are CURES-enrolled. If either is not yet in place, please let us know — both are gating prerequisites for any TRT prescribing under this SOP.

**Estimated review time:** 3 hours
**Target turnaround:** 21 calendar days from receipt (longer due to scope and clinical depth)

## 5. Review Logistics

### 5.1 Format and Delivery

All seven SOPs are delivered as Markdown source documents. We can provide DOCX export of any SOP for redline review if your preference is to work in Microsoft Word. Please indicate your format preference upon receipt.

### 5.2 Comment and Redline Workflow

For substantive revisions you propose during review, follow the change request workflow defined in SOP-001 §5.13:
- Operational changes route through the technical change request process (the MSO Owner manages these)
- Clinical changes are made directly by you in the source document with redlines or tracked changes; the MSO Owner incorporates your changes into the next version of the SOP

### 5.3 Synchronous Discussion

We anticipate at least three synchronous calls during this initial review:
- **Kickoff call (60 minutes, within 7 days of receipt):** Walk through the SOP system architecture, the MSO/PC boundary in §2.3, and the Provisional approval mechanism. Establish your preferences on visit modality, prescribing pathway, refill mechanics, and follow-up cadence so that subsequent SOP review is efficient.
- **Mid-review consolidation call (60 minutes, after you complete review of SOP-001, SOP-003, and SOP-006):** Discuss your proposed revisions on the foundational SOPs, alignment on clinical content, and any issues requiring iteration before signing.
- **Prescribing review call (60-90 minutes, focused on SOP-007 and its attachments):** Deep dive on the prescribing framework, controlled substance protocols, pharmacy architecture, and the four service-line attachments. SOP-007 has enough clinical substance that a dedicated discussion is warranted.

Additional calls are scheduled as needed.

### 5.4 Approval Mechanics

Each SOP has a defined signature block that you sign as Document Owner and Approving Authority for clinical SOPs (SOP-003, SOP-004, SOP-005, SOP-007) and as Approving Authority for the clinical components of hybrid SOPs (SOP-006). Signature is electronic per SOP-001 §5.7 (compliant with California Civil Code §1633.1 et seq., the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act). Wet signature is acceptable as an alternative if you prefer.

Upon your signature, the SOP transitions from Draft to Provisional or Approved per the SOP-001 §5.6 framework. Provisional status carries a 180-day maximum clock during which time full clinical review is completed and the SOP transitions to Approved.

### 5.5 Pre-Engagement Verifications for SOP-007

SOP-007 governs Schedule III controlled substance prescribing under your California DEA registration. Before SOP-007 can transition out of Draft status, the following must be verified:

- Active and unrestricted California DEA registration
- Active CURES enrollment
- OptiMantra EPCS identity proofing and two-factor authentication setup

If any of these is not yet in place, please flag during the kickoff call. We will coordinate the necessary steps before SOP-007 review begins in earnest. The other six SOPs do not depend on these verifications and can proceed in parallel.
