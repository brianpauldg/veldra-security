## Overview

This attachment documents the operational mechanics of lab cost management under Bloom Metabolics' bundled pricing model. Lab costs are absorbed into service tier pricing; patients pay a single price covering the visit, prescribing, and required labs.

## 2. Service Tier Lab Cost Allocation

Lab costs are a line item in the service tier pricing model:

| Service Tier | Included Labs | Estimated Annual Lab Cost per Patient |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Foundation | Pre-visit panel + 1 monitoring panel per year | $130-200 |
| Optimization | Pre-visit panel + 2 monitoring panels per year | $200-340 |
| Elite | Pre-visit panel + 4 monitoring panels per year + expanded panels | $340-560 |

The MSO Owner ensures that service tier pricing covers the estimated lab cost with sufficient margin. Pricing review occurs annually or when lab costs change materially.

---

## 3. Lab Cost Tracking Workflow

1. LabCorp invoices are received monthly
2. The MSO Owner reviews each invoice within 5 business days
3. Invoice line items are attributed to individual patients and service lines in the financial workbook
4. Monthly totals are compared against service tier revenue by service line
5. The lab cost ratio (lab cost / service tier revenue) is calculated monthly per service line

---

## 4. Lab Cost Watch Threshold

**Threshold:** If the lab cost ratio exceeds 15% of service tier revenue for any service line for two consecutive months, the MSO Owner initiates a response.

**Response options (escalating):**

1. **Panel optimization review:** Consult with the Covering Physician to evaluate whether any tests in the standard panels can be moved from "Required" to "Optional" or reduced in frequency without compromising clinical standards
2. **Monitoring cadence review:** Evaluate whether monitoring frequency can be reduced for stable patients (requires Covering Physician approval and SOP revision)
3. **Service tier pricing adjustment:** Increase the service tier price to restore margin, documented through the standard pricing review process
4. **Vendor renegotiation:** Renegotiate LabCorp contracted rates or evaluate alternative vendors (major SOP revision)

The 15% threshold is an operational watch level. It is not an automatic trigger for any specific action. The MSO Owner exercises judgment on the appropriate response.

---

## 5. Refund Handling for Unused Labs

- **Patient pays service tier but does not complete pre-visit draw within 30 days:** MSO Intake Staff contacts patient with reminders per [Attachment E](attachment-E-patient-lab-communication-templates.md) Template 2 and 3
- **Patient does not complete draw within 60 days:** Patient record moved to "Abandoned — No Lab Draw." Refund eligibility is evaluated per the patient-facing Terms of Service.
- **LabCorp charges for unused orders:** Minimal — LabCorp generally does not charge for requisitions that are not drawn. The operational concern is patient retention, not lab cost recovery.
- **Patient completes intake but cancels before visit:** Lab cost (if drawn) is a sunk cost absorbed by Bloom Metabolics. The service tier fee is subject to the refund terms in the patient-facing Terms of Service.

---

## 6. Elective and Exception Labs

Labs outside the standard panel for a service line (requested by the patient or ordered by the physician for non-standard clinical reasons) are handled as exceptions:

1. The MSO Owner is notified of the exception order
2. If the exception lab is within the LabCorp standard menu and the cost is under $50, it is absorbed into the service tier price
3. If the exception lab is outside the standard menu or the cost exceeds $50, the MSO Owner evaluates whether to absorb the cost or bill the patient separately
4. If billed separately, the patient is informed before the order is placed and consents to the additional charge
5. Exception labs are tracked separately in the financial workbook for cost monitoring
