## C.1 Service Line Scope

Sexual health assessment and treatment for adult patients (18+) who are California residents, delivered via the Bloom Metabolics telehealth platform.

- **FDA-approved generics (tadalafil, sildenafil):** Prescribed and dispensed through retail or mail-order pharmacies. These are non-controlled substances.
- **Compounded PT-141 (bremelanotide):** Prescribed through a Bloom-contracted 503A or 503B compounding pharmacy partner. Compounded formulation; no FDA-approved subcutaneous bremelanotide product is currently available through the Bloom formulary.

Eligibility screening is governed by [SOP-003 — Clinical Eligibility Screening](../../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/attachments/attachment-A-service-line-eligibility-matrix.md). Pre-visit lab requirements, if any, are defined in [SOP-006, Attachment A](../../SOP-006-lab-ordering-and-review/attachments/attachment-A-pre-visit-lab-panels-by-service-line.md).

## C.2 Treatment Model

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define whether the Sexual Health service line uses the three-phase Initiation/Titration/Maintenance model described in SOP-007 §5.2, or a simplified continuous/as-needed model.]`

Industry-typical context for the physician's consideration:

- **PDE5 inhibitors (as-needed dosing):** Do not follow a conventional titration arc. The patient is prescribed an as-needed dose; dose adjustment occurs based on efficacy and tolerability at follow-up, but there is no structured titration phase in the traditional sense.
- **PDE5 inhibitors (daily tadalafil):** May have a brief titration component (e.g., starting at 2.5 mg daily, increasing to 5 mg daily if tolerated and needed).
- **PT-141:** Typically prescribed as-needed. No loading or titration phase is standard.

The physician should specify whether Sexual Health patients follow a defined treatment model or proceed under an as-needed prescribing framework with periodic reassessment.

## C.8 Patient Education

The following patient education topics must be addressed and documented before or at the time of first prescription. Education materials are delivered through the patient portal and reinforced during the synchronous visit per [SOP-005](../../SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit/SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit.md).

### PDE5 Inhibitor Education (Tadalafil, Sildenafil)

- **Nitrate absolute contraindication:** The patient must understand that combining PDE5 inhibitors with nitrates (including recreational amyl nitrite/poppers) can cause life-threatening hypotension. This must be documented as acknowledged by the patient.
- **Priapism emergency:** Erection lasting longer than 4 hours requires immediate emergency department evaluation. Delayed treatment risks permanent tissue damage.
- **Timing and food interactions:** Sildenafil efficacy may be reduced by high-fat meals; tadalafil is less affected.
- **Alcohol:** Moderate alcohol consumption may increase hypotensive effects.
- **Storage:** Room temperature, away from moisture and heat.

### PT-141 Education

- **Injection technique:** Self-administration of subcutaneous injection. Reference [SOP-004](../../SOP-004-injection-education/SOP-004-injection-education.md) if applicable for injection training protocol.
- **Blood pressure awareness:** Patient should be counseled that transient blood pressure elevation is expected and typically self-limited.
- **Nausea management:** Most common side effect. Patient should be advised on timing relative to meals and symptom expectations.
- **Hyperpigmentation disclosure:** Repeated use may cause focal skin darkening. Patient should report any new or changing skin pigmentation.
- **Storage:** Per compounding pharmacy dispensing instructions. Typically refrigerated.
- **Timing:** Administer at least 45 minutes before anticipated activity.
