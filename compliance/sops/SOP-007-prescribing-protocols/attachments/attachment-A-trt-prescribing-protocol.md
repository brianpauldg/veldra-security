## A.1 Service Line Scope

**Therapy:** Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)

**Patient population:** Adult male patients, age 18 or older, who are residents of California and who have been determined clinically eligible per [SOP-003 (Clinical Eligibility Screening)](../../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md) and who have completed a synchronous telehealth visit per [SOP-005 (Synchronous Telehealth Visit)](../../SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit/SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit.md).

**Controlled substance classification:** Testosterone cypionate is a Schedule III controlled substance under federal law (21 U.S.C. 812) and California Health & Safety Code 11056. All prescribing under this attachment is subject to Schedule III requirements as defined in [SOP-007 section 5.6](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md).

**Default product:** FDA-approved testosterone cypionate (e.g., Depo-Testosterone or AB-rated generic) dispensed via Strive Pharmacy (compounding pharmacy partner).

**Exception pathway — compounded testosterone:** Compounded testosterone cypionate may be prescribed only when the Covering Physician documents a patient-specific medical necessity that cannot be met by an FDA-approved product at an available concentration or volume. Compounded testosterone is an exception, not a default. Documentation requirements are defined in section A.3 below and in [SOP-007 section 5.9](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md).

## A.2 Three-Phase Treatment Model

TRT at Bloom Metabolics follows a three-phase treatment model. Each phase has distinct monitoring cadences, visit requirements, and prescribing rules.

### A.2.1 Initiation Phase (Weeks 1-12)

**Purpose:** Establish baseline response to testosterone replacement. Evaluate tolerability, initial lab response, and symptom trajectory.

**Starting regimen:**

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the following for the initiation phase:

- Starting dose (mg per injection)
- Testosterone cypionate concentration (e.g., 200 mg/mL)
- Injection frequency (e.g., weekly, biweekly)
- Route of administration (e.g., intramuscular gluteal, subcutaneous)
- Syringe/needle specifications if relevant to pharmacy dispensing
- Whether the starting dose is uniform or weight-adjusted

Industry-typical starting regimen for telehealth TRT:
- Testosterone cypionate 200 mg/mL
- 100-200 mg intramuscular injection every 7-14 days
- Some providers start at 100 mg weekly and titrate based on labs
- Subcutaneous injection is increasingly common at lower volumes]`

**Monitoring:**

- First monitoring labs drawn at 6-8 weeks post-initiation per [SOP-006 (Lab Ordering & Review)](../../SOP-006-lab-ordering-and-review/SOP-006-lab-ordering-and-review.md) and [SOP-006 Attachment B (Post-Visit Monitoring Lab Cadence)](../../SOP-006-lab-ordering-and-review/attachments/attachment-B-post-visit-monitoring-lab-cadence.md)
- Labs drawn at trough (immediately before next scheduled injection) unless physician specifies otherwise
- Lab panel per [SOP-006 Attachment A](../../SOP-006-lab-ordering-and-review/attachments/attachment-A-pre-visit-lab-panels-by-service-line.md)

**End-of-phase visit:**

- Synchronous telehealth visit required at the end of the initiation phase (approximately week 10-12)
- Visit follows [SOP-005](../../SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit/SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit.md) protocol
- Purpose: review 6-8 week labs, assess symptom response, evaluate tolerability, determine whether to continue at current dose or enter titration
- Prescribing decision at this visit determines whether the patient advances to the titration phase or requires dose modification before advancing

**Visit cadence:**

## A.3 Schedule III Prescribing Specifics

Testosterone cypionate is a Schedule III controlled substance. The following rules apply in addition to all general prescribing requirements in [SOP-007](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md).

### A.3.1 Prescription Parameters

- **Initial prescription duration:** `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the duration of the initial prescription. Industry typical: 30-day or 90-day supply depending on physician preference and pharmacy partner dispensing model.]`
- **Refills authorized per prescription:** `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define number of refills. Federal maximum for Schedule III: 5 refills within 6 months of the date of issue (21 CFR 1306.22).]`
- **Prescription validity period:** 6 months from date of issue per federal Schedule III rules. No prescription may be filled or refilled more than 6 months after the date of issue.
- **New prescription required:** After 5 refills or 6 months from date of issue, whichever occurs first. New prescription requires a synchronous visit per [SOP-007 section 5.6](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md).

### A.3.2 CURES Reporting

- CURES (Controlled Substance Utilization Review and Evaluation System) check is required at every new prescription and every refill authorization per California Business & Professions Code 11165.4
- CURES check must be completed by the prescribing physician or authorized delegate before authorizing any fill
- CURES report must be reviewed for: concurrent controlled substance prescriptions from other providers, patterns suggesting misuse or diversion, aberrant fill patterns
- CURES check is documented in OptiMantra per [SOP-007](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md) documentation requirements

### A.3.3 Compounded Testosterone Exception Documentation

When compounded testosterone is prescribed in lieu of an FDA-approved product, the following must be documented in the patient record:

1. The specific clinical reason the FDA-approved product is unsuitable for this patient (e.g., allergy to a component, medically necessary concentration not commercially available)
2. The compounding pharmacy (503A or 503B) and its accreditation status
3. Patient acknowledgment that the prescribed product is compounded and not FDA-approved
4. Periodic re-evaluation of whether the patient can transition to an FDA-approved product (minimum every 6 months)

## A.7 Patient Education

### A.7.1 Education Protocol Reference

Patient education for TRT is delivered per SOP-004 (Patient Communication & Education) Protocol 001, if applicable. If SOP-004 has not yet been finalized, the Covering Physician ensures that the following topics are addressed during the initiation phase visit and documented in the visit note.

### A.7.2 Required Education Topics

The following topics must be covered with every TRT patient prior to or during the initiation phase:

1. **Schedule III controlled substance status:** Testosterone is a federally controlled substance. The patient must understand that prescriptions are subject to monitoring (CURES), refill limitations, and regulatory requirements that differ from non-controlled medications.
2. **Storage and handling:** Testosterone cypionate is stored at controlled room temperature (68-77 degrees F). Protect from light. Do not freeze. Dispose of unused medication per pharmacy instructions; do not share or transfer medication to another person.
3. **Injection technique:** Self-injection training provided during the initial visit or via standardized instructional materials. Includes site selection, preparation, injection procedure, and post-injection monitoring.
4. **Lab monitoring cadence:** Patient understands that regular lab draws are required throughout treatment. Labs are drawn at trough (before the next injection). Missed or delayed labs may result in held refills.
5. **Dose stability expectations:** Therapeutic testosterone levels are achieved over weeks to months, not days. Dose adjustments are made based on lab values and clinical assessment, not subjective symptom response alone. Patients should not expect immediate symptom resolution.
6. **Potential side effects:** `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the side effects to be discussed. Industry typical: polycythemia, acne, mood changes, testicular atrophy, reduced fertility, edema, sleep apnea exacerbation, cardiovascular considerations.]`
7. **When to contact Bloom Metabolics:** Patient is instructed to contact the practice if experiencing: chest pain, shortness of breath, leg swelling or pain, severe headache, vision changes, mood disturbance, injection site infection, or any other concerning symptom. Emergency symptoms require 911.
8. **Treatment duration expectations:** TRT is typically an indefinite therapy. Discontinuation leads to return of hypogonadal symptoms. The physician and patient will reassess the treatment plan at each synchronous visit.
