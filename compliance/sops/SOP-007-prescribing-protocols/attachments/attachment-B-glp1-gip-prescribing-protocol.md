## B.1 Service Line Scope

**Therapy:** GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist therapy for weight management

**Patient population:** Adult patients, age 18 or older, who are residents of California, who meet BMI or weight-related comorbidity criteria per [SOP-003 (Clinical Eligibility Screening)](../../SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening/SOP-003-clinical-eligibility-screening.md), and who have completed a synchronous telehealth visit per [SOP-005 (Synchronous Telehealth Visit)](../../SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit/SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit.md).

**Controlled substance classification:** GLP-1/GIP receptor agonists are not controlled substances. Prescribing follows the non-controlled substance pathway defined in [SOP-007 section 5.5](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md).

**Default pathway — FDA-approved branded products:**

- **Semaglutide:** Wegovy (weight management indication) or Ozempic (type 2 diabetes indication, off-label for weight management if Wegovy unavailable)
- **Tirzepatide:** Zepbound (weight management indication) or Mounjaro (type 2 diabetes indication, off-label for weight management if Zepbound unavailable)

FDA-approved branded products are the default prescribing pathway. Pharmacy routing: retail pharmacy or mail-order pharmacy; patient is responsible for fill and payment. Bloom Metabolics does not stock, dispense, or ship GLP-1/GIP medications under the default pathway.

**Cost transparency:** Branded GLP-1/GIP medications are typically $1,000-$1,500 per month (2026 pricing) without insurance coverage. Patients must be informed of expected cost before prescribing. Insurance coverage and manufacturer savings programs vary. Bloom Metabolics does not guarantee coverage or pricing.

**Exception pathway — compounded GLP-1:** Compounded semaglutide or tirzepatide may be prescribed only under the conditions defined in section B.4 below and in [SOP-007 section 5.9](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md).

## B.3 Three-Phase Treatment Model

### B.3.1 Initiation Phase (Weeks 1-4)

**Purpose:** Introduce the GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist at the lowest recommended dose. Assess GI tolerability. Establish injection technique and patient adherence.

**Starting dose:**

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Confirm starting doses per FDA-approved prescribing information.

Industry-typical starting doses per FDA PI:
- Semaglutide (Wegovy): 0.25 mg subcutaneous injection once weekly for 4 weeks
- Tirzepatide (Zepbound): 2.5 mg subcutaneous injection once weekly for 4 weeks

Physician must confirm adherence to FDA-approved titration schedules or define any modifications.]`

**GI tolerability monitoring:**

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the tolerability monitoring protocol during the initiation phase.

Industry-typical monitoring considerations:
- Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation are the most common adverse effects during initiation and dose escalation
- Severity grading framework (mild/moderate/severe) for GI symptoms
- Criteria for holding at current dose vs. proceeding with titration
- Criteria for dose reduction or temporary hold
- Patient self-reporting cadence (e.g., secure message at week 2 if experiencing significant GI symptoms)
- Dietary guidance to reduce GI symptoms (smaller meals, avoid high-fat foods, adequate hydration)]`

**End-of-phase visit:**

- Synchronous telehealth visit at approximately week 4
- Purpose: assess GI tolerability, review adherence, confirm readiness for dose escalation
- Prescribing decision at this visit determines whether the patient advances to the first dose escalation step

### B.3.2 Titration Phase (Weeks 4-20)

**Purpose:** Escalate dose per FDA-approved schedule toward the maintenance dose. Active monitoring for tolerability and clinical response.

**Dose escalation schedule:**

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Confirm dose escalation schedules per FDA PI and define rules for escalation, hold, and slowing.

FDA-approved escalation schedules (for reference):

Semaglutide (Wegovy):
- Weeks 1-4: 0.25 mg weekly
- Weeks 5-8: 0.5 mg weekly
- Weeks 9-12: 1.0 mg weekly
- Weeks 13-16: 1.7 mg weekly
- Week 17+: 2.4 mg weekly (maintenance dose)

Tirzepatide (Zepbound):
- Weeks 1-4: 2.5 mg weekly
- Weeks 5-8: 5 mg weekly
- Weeks 9-12: 7.5 mg weekly (may maintain here or escalate)
- Weeks 13-16: 10 mg weekly
- Weeks 17-20: 12.5 mg weekly
- Week 21+: 15 mg weekly (maximum dose)

Physician must define:
- Criteria for proceeding with each escalation step (tolerability adequate, patient adherent)
- Criteria for holding at current dose (GI intolerance at current dose, patient request)
- Criteria for slowing the escalation schedule (e.g., extending each step from 4 weeks to 6-8 weeks)
- Criteria for dose reduction (persistent intolerance despite hold period)
- Whether a patient may remain at a sub-maximal dose indefinitely if achieving adequate response
- Minimum weight loss or clinical response expected at each dose level (if any)]`

**Monitoring cadence:**

- Synchronous or async check-in at each dose escalation step per [SOP-007 section 5.5](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md)
- Labs per [SOP-006 Attachment B](../../SOP-006-lab-ordering-and-review/attachments/attachment-B-post-visit-monitoring-lab-cadence.md) at `[CLINICAL — PENDING]` intervals during titration (industry typical: every 3 months)
- Weight documented at each encounter

### B.3.3 Maintenance Phase

**Purpose:** Ongoing GLP-1/GIP therapy at the maintenance dose (or the highest tolerated dose achieving adequate clinical response).

**Entry criteria:** Patient is stable at the target dose with acceptable tolerability for `[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN]` minimum duration (industry typical: 4-8 weeks at maintenance dose without dose changes).

**Monitoring cadence:**

## B.4 Compounded GLP-1 Exception Pathway

### B.4.1 Regulatory Context

As of the date of this document, semaglutide and tirzepatide are NOT on the FDA Drug Shortage List. Compounding of commercially available, non-shortage drugs is subject to stricter regulatory requirements under section 503A and 503B of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act.

Compounded GLP-1/GIP products may only be prescribed when there is a documented, patient-specific medical necessity that cannot be met by the commercially available FDA-approved product. Cost savings alone does not constitute medical necessity.

### B.4.2 Medical Necessity Criteria

`[CLINICAL — PENDING COVERING PHYSICIAN: Define the specific clinical criteria under which a compounded GLP-1/GIP product may be prescribed. The following scaffolding reflects potential criteria; the physician must define the operative criteria.

Potential medical necessity criteria under 503A:
- Documented allergy or adverse reaction to a specific inactive ingredient in the FDA-approved product that is not present in the compounded formulation
- Need for a dose or concentration not commercially available (e.g., intermediate dose during titration)
- Other patient-specific medical reason documented in the chart

Criteria that do NOT constitute medical necessity:
- Patient preference for lower cost
- Insurance non-coverage of the branded product
- General unavailability at the patient's preferred pharmacy (unless a true supply shortage is documented)
- Patient request without clinical justification]`

### B.4.3 Documentation Requirements

When a compounded GLP-1/GIP product is prescribed under this exception pathway:

1. The prescribing physician documents the specific medical necessity in the patient record, including why the FDA-approved product is unsuitable for this individual patient
2. The compounding pharmacy partner (503A or 503B) is identified and its accreditation/licensing status is confirmed
3. The patient signs an acknowledgment that the prescribed product is compounded and not FDA-approved, including a statement that the patient understands the regulatory distinction
4. The prescription is routed to the designated compounding pharmacy partner per [SOP-007 section 5.9](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md)

### B.4.4 Periodic Re-evaluation

- Compounded GLP-1/GIP prescriptions are re-evaluated at minimum every 90 days
- At each re-evaluation, the prescribing physician documents whether the medical necessity for compounding persists
- If the medical necessity no longer exists (e.g., the allergy was to an ingredient that has been reformulated out of the branded product), the patient is transitioned to the FDA-approved product
- If the FDA adds semaglutide or tirzepatide to the Drug Shortage List, the re-evaluation cadence and medical necessity threshold may be adjusted per [SOP-007 section 5.9](../SOP-007-prescribing-protocols.md)

## B.10 Patient Education

### B.10.1 Education Protocol Reference

Patient education for GLP-1/GIP therapy is delivered per SOP-004 (Patient Communication & Education) Protocol 002, if applicable. If SOP-004 has not yet been finalized, the Covering Physician ensures that the following topics are addressed during the initiation phase visit and documented in the visit note.

### B.10.2 Required Education Topics

The following topics must be covered with every GLP-1/GIP patient prior to or during the initiation phase:

1. **GI management expectations:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation are common, particularly during initiation and dose escalation. Symptoms typically improve over time. Dietary modifications (smaller meals, reduced fat intake, adequate hydration) may mitigate symptoms. Patients should report persistent or severe GI symptoms rather than self-discontinuing.
2. **Dose titration patience:** The dose is increased gradually over weeks to months per the FDA-approved schedule. Patients should not expect rapid weight loss at the starting dose. Each dose step must be tolerated before escalation.
3. **Cost expectations:** Branded GLP-1/GIP medications are high-cost therapies. Patients are financially responsible for medication costs. Insurance coverage is variable and not guaranteed. Bloom Metabolics will assist with prior authorization documentation where possible but does not control coverage decisions.
4. **Storage requirements:** Medications must be refrigerated per product-specific instructions (see sections B.5 and B.6). Improper storage may reduce efficacy. Patients should not use medication that has been frozen or stored outside recommended conditions.
5. **Injection technique:** Self-injection training provided during the initial visit or via standardized instructional materials. Includes site selection (abdomen, thigh, upper arm), rotation, preparation, and disposal.
6. **Weight regain on discontinuation:** Patients are informed that weight regain is expected if therapy is discontinued (see section B.9). Treatment is anticipated to be long-term. Discontinuation decisions should be made in consultation with the prescribing physician.
7. **When to contact Bloom Metabolics:** Patients are instructed to contact the practice if experiencing: severe or persistent nausea/vomiting, severe abdominal pain (may indicate pancreatitis), signs of allergic reaction (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing), gallbladder symptoms (right upper quadrant pain), symptoms of hypoglycemia (if diabetic or on concurrent diabetes medications), mental health changes (mood, suicidal ideation). Emergency symptoms require 911.
8. **Monitoring cadence:** Patients understand that periodic lab draws and follow-up visits are required throughout treatment per [SOP-006](../../SOP-006-lab-ordering-and-review/SOP-006-lab-ordering-and-review.md).

### B.10.3 Informed Consent

GLP-1/GIP-specific informed consent is documented per [SOP-005 Attachment E (Informed Consent — GLP-1/GIP)](../../SOP-005-synchronous-telehealth-visit/attachments/attachment-E-informed-consent-glp1-gip.md).
