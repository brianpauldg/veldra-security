Frame 2, Definition

What is NAD+?

Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide. A coenzyme found in every living cell. Central to how your cells produce energy, repair DNA, and regulate metabolism.

Without it, cellular function stops.

BLOOM
Frame 3, Why It Matters

Why researchers are paying attention.

NAD+ levels naturally decline with age.

Studies suggest this decline correlates with reduced metabolic efficiency, slower cellular repair, and changes in energy regulation.

By age 50, NAD+ levels may be roughly half of youthful baseline.

Why it's a longevity research focus.

Researchers studying healthspan are exploring whether supporting NAD+ pathways could influence how cells age, repair, and metabolize.
