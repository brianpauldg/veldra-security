## The Angle
The "metabolic damage" narrative is oversimplified — metabolism does not break. But metabolic adaptation is real: resting metabolic rate decreases beyond what body composition changes alone would predict, leptin drops disproportionately, and energy expenditure becomes more efficient in ways that favor weight regain. GLP-1 medications interact with these adaptive mechanisms in ways most content ignores entirely. The clinical question is not whether adaptation occurs — it does — but whether GLP-1 therapy modifies the trajectory of adaptation compared to caloric restriction alone, and what that means for long-term outcomes.
