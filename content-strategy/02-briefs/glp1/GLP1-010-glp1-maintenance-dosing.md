## The Angle
Most GLP-1 content focuses on getting ON the medication — titration schedules, initial weight loss, side effect management during the first months. Almost none addresses what responsible long-term management looks like after the active weight loss phase. Maintenance dosing is where the provider relationship earns its ongoing value: finding the lowest effective dose, monitoring for sustained efficacy, adjusting based on body composition changes, and having honest conversations about treatment duration. This is the article that shifts the narrative from "how to start" to "what comes after you've arrived."

- H3: Why dose reduction after target weight is a clinical consideration

- Content note: The nuance moment. Dose reduction is not always appropriate. Some patients require the full therapeutic dose for maintenance. Others may maintain on a lower dose. This is why it requires clinical monitoring, not patient self-adjustment. (Cross-link GLP1-005 microdosing for contrast.)

### H2: What Long-Term Monitoring Should Include
- H3: Weight and body composition tracking — not just the scale
- H3: Metabolic markers — lipids, glucose, inflammatory markers
- H3: Side effect reassessment at maintenance doses
- H3: Lean mass monitoring over time (cross-link GLP1-002)
- Content note: Monitoring during maintenance is different from monitoring during active weight loss. The questions change. The frequency may change. The provider should be adjusting the monitoring protocol to match the treatment phase.
