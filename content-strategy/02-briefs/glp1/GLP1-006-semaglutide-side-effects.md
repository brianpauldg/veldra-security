## The Angle
Most side effect content falls into one of two categories: alarmist listicles designed for clicks, or dismissive reassurances designed for conversions. Neither serves the patient. The clinical reality is that semaglutide side effects are predominantly gastrointestinal, dose-dependent, and manageable with proper titration and monitoring — which is precisely why the provider relationship matters. The moment of pushback: some patients will experience side effects significant enough to warrant dose adjustment or discontinuation, and a responsible provider acknowledges this rather than minimizing it.
