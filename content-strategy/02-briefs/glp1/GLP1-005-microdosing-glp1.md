## The Angle
The internet is full of anecdotal microdosing protocols — patients splitting pens, using sub-therapeutic doses, titrating slower than label recommendations. The clinical evidence for microdosing GLP-1 medications is thin, but the patient interest is real and growing. Most providers either ignore the trend or condemn it without engaging with the underlying rationale. The honest position is more nuanced: there are legitimate clinical reasons a provider might prescribe lower-than-standard doses, but self-directed microdosing without medical oversight carries specific risks that patients should understand.
