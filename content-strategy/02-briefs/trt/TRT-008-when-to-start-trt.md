## The Angle
The honest answer to "when should I start TRT?" is "it depends." But that is not where the conversation should end — it is where it should begin. Most content either pushes men toward treatment prematurely or tells them to "try lifestyle changes first" without defining what "first" means or how long to wait. This piece provides the actual clinical decision tree: labs, symptoms, lifestyle optimization timeline, fertility considerations, risk factors, and the threshold where waiting itself becomes the less responsible choice. This is the framework a good clinician walks through before writing a prescription — and the conversation most men never get.

## Compliance Flags
- Do not imply that lifestyle optimization is futile — frame as "necessary first step with a defined endpoint"
- Do not suggest that any man below a certain threshold automatically needs TRT
- Avoid implying that waiting is always harmful — frame the cost of waiting as one factor in a multi-factor decision
- Do not position TRT as a performance-enhancing shortcut — frame as medical treatment for diagnosed deficiency
- Required disclaimer: "The decision to start TRT should be made collaboratively with a licensed provider after thorough evaluation. This framework is educational and does not replace clinical judgment."
