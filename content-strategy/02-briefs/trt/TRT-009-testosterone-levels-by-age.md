## The Angle
This is a link-bait and featured snippet opportunity. Every health site publishes a testosterone-by-age table, but few explain how those ranges were derived or why they may not serve the modern patient. The reference ranges most labs use were established on populations that included men with undiagnosed comorbidities, and they have not been meaningfully updated to reflect the well-documented secular decline in population testosterone levels. A 40-year-old in 2026 has, on average, lower testosterone than a 40-year-old in 1990 — and the reference range treats them identically. This piece provides the data visualization men are searching for, then layers on the clinical context that turns a commodity table into a trust-building asset.
