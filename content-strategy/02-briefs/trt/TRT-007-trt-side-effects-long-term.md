## The Angle
Long-term TRT safety content falls into two camps: fear-mongering that cites decade-old, methodologically flawed studies, or hand-waving that dismisses all risk as manageable. Neither serves the patient. The TRAVERSE trial (2023) — the first large-scale, randomized, placebo-controlled cardiovascular safety trial for TRT — changed the evidence landscape meaningfully. This piece presents the actual data: hematocrit elevation is real and manageable, cardiovascular risk is not the apocalypse earlier studies suggested, prostate risk has been overstated for decades, and the side effects that matter most are the ones that go unmonitored. The nuance is the angle.

## Compliance Flags
- Do not state that TRT is "safe" in absolute terms — use "current evidence suggests" and "when properly monitored"
- Do not dismiss cardiovascular risk entirely — TRAVERSE showed non-inferiority, not superiority
- Do not claim TRT does not affect the prostate — frame the saturation model as "current understanding suggests" with "ongoing monitoring remains appropriate"
- Avoid positioning monitoring as Bloom's unique advantage in a way that implies other providers don't monitor
- Required disclaimer: "Long-term TRT requires ongoing medical supervision. All treatment decisions should be made with a licensed provider who monitors relevant health markers."
