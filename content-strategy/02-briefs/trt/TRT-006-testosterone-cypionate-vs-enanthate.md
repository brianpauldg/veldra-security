## The Angle
This is one of the most searched TRT comparison queries, and most articles conclude with "they are basically the same." That is largely true from a pharmacokinetic standpoint — the half-life difference is approximately one day. But the articles stop there. What actually matters to the patient injecting twice a week is carrier oil (cottonseed vs sesame vs MCT), injection volume, post-injection pain, and regulatory availability. These are compliance factors, not pharmacological ones, and they determine whether a man stays on protocol or starts skipping doses. Bloom uses compounded testosterone cypionate in MCT oil — and this piece explains why that specific choice was made.

## Compliance Flags
- Do not claim one ester is medically superior to the other without evidence
- Avoid implying that brand-name formulations are inferior to compounded — frame as "different options with different trade-offs"
- Do not make claims about MCT carrier oil superiority beyond reduced viscosity — PIP reduction is patient-reported, not clinically validated at scale
- Required disclaimer: "Testosterone formulation and ester selection should be determined by a licensed prescriber based on individual patient factors."
