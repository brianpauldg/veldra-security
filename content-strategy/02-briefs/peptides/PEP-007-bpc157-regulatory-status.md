## The Angle

BPC-157 is the peptide most patients ask about, and its regulatory story serves as a useful case study for understanding the entire FDA compounding framework. It moved categories. It generated public comment. It became a flashpoint in the broader debate about compounding pharmacy regulation. If you understand what happened to BPC-157 and why, you understand the mechanics that will determine the future of every peptide on the FDA's evaluation list. The molecule itself is interesting. The regulatory story is instructive.

### H2: What BPC-157 Actually Is
- **H3: The Molecule**
  - Body Protection Compound — a synthetic analog of a peptide isolated from human gastric juice
  - 15-amino-acid sequence
  - Not identical to the endogenous peptide — an important distinction
  - Stable form (BPC-157 acetate salt) vs. arginine salt variants
- **H3: The Research Base**
  - Extensive preclinical literature: tendon healing, muscle healing, gut mucosal protection in animal models
  - Dr. Predrag Sikiric's laboratory at the University of Zagreb as the primary research group
  - The concentration-of-research concern: when most studies come from one lab
  - No completed Phase II or Phase III human clinical trials as of the knowledge cutoff
  - The honest framing: a large preclinical evidence base with a notable absence of controlled human data
