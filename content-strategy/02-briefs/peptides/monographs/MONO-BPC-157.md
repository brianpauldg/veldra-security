## Identity & Classification
- Full name: Body Protection Compound-157
- Peptide sequence: 15 amino acids (Gly-Glu-Pro-Pro-Pro-Gly-Lys-Pro-Ala-Asp-Asp-Ala-Gly-Leu-Val), partial sequence of human gastric juice protein BPC
- Classification: Synthetic analog of a gastric peptide / cytoprotective peptide
- Discovery: Isolated from human gastric juice and developed primarily by Dr. Predrag Sikiric's research group at the University of Zagreb, Croatia, beginning in the early 1990s
- Common alternate names: BPC 157, Bepecin, PL 14736 (pentadecapeptide)

## Mechanism of Action
- Cytoprotective pathways: Interacts with the NO (nitric oxide) system, promotes angiogenesis, and modulates growth factor expression
- Tendon and ligament healing: Accelerates tendon-to-bone healing, increases collagen organization, and promotes fibroblast activity (in animal models)
- Gastrointestinal protection: Protects against various GI insults (NSAIDs, alcohol, stress-induced lesions) in animal models — consistent with its origin as a gastric juice peptide
- Neuroprotective signals: Protection against dopaminergic neurotoxicity and peripheral nerve healing in rat models
- Established: Cytoprotective and wound-healing mechanisms well-characterized in extensive animal models
- Hypothesized: Systemic tissue healing, tendon/ligament repair, GI healing in humans
- Incomplete: Virtually no published human interventional data. The entire evidence base is preclinical.

## Bloom Evidence Tier Rating
Tier 3 — Extensive preclinical data from a concentrated research group. Strong mechanistic plausibility. No completed Phase II/III human trials. The evidence quality gap is significant: the preclinical profile is one of the most robust in the peptide space, but the absence of human interventional data means clinical efficacy is unproven.

Justification: Dr. Sikiric's group has published over 100 papers on BPC-157, demonstrating consistent effects across multiple animal models and injury types. The research concentration (single group, single institution) is both a strength (consistency) and a limitation (independent replication). The complete absence of completed human interventional trials after 30+ years of research is notable and should be addressed honestly.

## Human Clinical Research
- No completed Phase II or Phase III human interventional trials as of May 2026
- One Phase II trial (PL 14736 for inflammatory bowel disease) was initiated but results were not published in peer-reviewed literature
- Anecdotal: Extensive patient-reported outcomes in online communities (injury recovery, gut healing) — these are self-reported, uncontrolled, and subject to confirmation bias
- Clinical relevance: The gap between preclinical promise and human validation is the defining feature of BPC-157's evidence profile
- Limitations: 30+ years of preclinical research without human trial completion raises legitimate questions about translational barriers

## Animal & Mechanistic Research
- Tendon healing: Accelerated Achilles tendon healing in rats (multiple studies)
- Muscle healing: Improved muscle healing after transection and crush injuries in rat models
- GI protection: Extensive data on protection against NSAID-induced gastric lesions, alcohol-induced GI damage, and stress ulcers
- Bone healing: Acceleration of fracture healing and bone-tendon junction repair
- Neural healing: Peripheral nerve repair and protection against neurotoxicity
- Extrapolation limits: The most commonly cited concern is the concentration of research in a single laboratory. While the data is internally consistent, independent replication across institutions is limited.

## Regulatory Status (as of May 2026)
- Current FDA category status: Under active FDA evaluation. BPC-157 has been placed in a restricted category for 503B outsourcing facilities. 503A status varies by state.
- 503A compoundability: Status varies by state; some state pharmacy boards permit 503A compounding with valid prescription, others have restricted it
- 503B compoundability: Restricted — removed from bulk drug substance list for 503B outsourcing facilities
- Recent regulatory changes: FDA enforcement action in 2024-2025 targeting 503B compounders producing BPC-157
- Pending FDA actions: Ongoing category evaluation
- International: Available in some international jurisdictions; not approved as a pharmaceutical in any major regulatory framework

## Clinical Considerations
- Theoretical benefits: Tissue healing (tendon, muscle, bone, GI), cytoprotection, anti-inflammatory effects
- Known contraindications: None established in human studies (insufficient human data)
- Drug interactions: Theoretical interactions with anticoagulants (angiogenesis promotion) and NSAIDs (cytoprotective counteraction)
- Monitoring: Standard inflammation markers, injury-specific imaging/assessment
- Population-specific: Athletes and patients with soft tissue injuries represent the largest use case; GI patients with inflammatory conditions represent a secondary use case
