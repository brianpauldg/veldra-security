## Identity & Classification
- Full name: Selank (TP-7)
- Peptide sequence: Heptapeptide — Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro (synthetic analog of the immunomodulatory peptide Tuftsin with the addition of a Pro-Gly-Pro sequence for metabolic stability)
- Classification: Synthetic Tuftsin analog / Anxiolytic peptide / Nootropic candidate
- Discovery: Developed at the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences in collaboration with the V.V. Zakusov Research Institute of Pharmacology, Moscow. Approved as a medication in Russia for anxiety and nootropic applications.
- Common alternate names: TP-7, Selanc

## Mechanism of Action
- GABA modulation: Selank appears to modulate GABAergic neurotransmission — increasing the expression of GABA-A receptor subunits, which may contribute to anxiolytic effects
- BDNF expression: Some studies report increased brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) expression, a key molecule in neuroplasticity and cognitive function
- Enkephalin modulation: Reported effects on enkephalin degradation, potentially affecting endogenous opioid signaling
- Immunomodulatory properties: As a Tuftsin analog, Selank retains some immunomodulatory activity — Tuftsin is a natural immunostimulatory peptide
- Established: Anxiolytic effects demonstrated in Russian clinical studies and approved by Russian regulatory authorities
- Hypothesized: Cognitive enhancement, neuroplasticity support, immune modulation in Western populations
- Incomplete: Western-standard clinical validation, mechanism of anxiolytic action (GABA modulation is reported but pathway characterization is incomplete), pharmacokinetics in diverse populations

## Bloom Evidence Tier Rating
Tier 4 — Approved medication in Russia with clinical use history. However, Western clinical trial data meeting FDA/EMA standards is absent. The Russian clinical evidence supports efficacy for anxiety, but the publication standards, trial design rigor, and transparency do not consistently meet Western benchmarks. Selank is one of very few peptides that is actually approved as a medication anywhere — but "approved in Russia" and "validated by Western clinical trial standards" are different evidentiary claims.

Justification: Selank's evidence profile is unusual. It has more real-world clinical use than almost any peptide in this library — it is a prescribed medication in Russia. But the clinical trial data supporting that approval has not been replicated in Western-standard trials (randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, adequately powered, pre-registered). The mechanism of action is partially characterized but not fully elucidated. The anxiolytic effects are consistent across Russian publications, but independent Western validation is the critical gap.

## Human Clinical Research
- Russian clinical trials: Multiple studies demonstrating anxiolytic effects comparable to benzodiazepines without sedation, cognitive impairment, or dependence
- Zozulya AA, et al. Studies on Selank's effects on anxiety and cognitive function in patients with generalized anxiety disorder
- Seredenin SB, et al. Research on Selank's mechanism in anxiety and stress adaptation
- Russian regulatory approval: Approved as an intranasal anxiolytic medication by the Russian Ministry of Health
- Limitations: No ClinicalTrials.gov-registered trials. Western peer-reviewed publications are limited. Russian clinical trial standards for approval differ from FDA/EMA requirements. Sample sizes in published trials are generally small.
- Clinical relevance: Real-world clinical use in Russia provides a safety signal (if a medication causes serious adverse effects at scale, it tends to become apparent). But this is not equivalent to Western Phase III validation.

## Animal & Mechanistic Research
- Anxiolytic effects demonstrated in multiple behavioral models (elevated plus maze, open field, etc.)
- GABA-A receptor subunit expression changes observed in rat brain tissue
- BDNF expression increases in hippocampal and cortical tissue in animal models
- Immunomodulatory effects consistent with Tuftsin analog activity
- Extrapolation limits: Animal behavioral models for anxiety have variable predictive validity for human anxiolytic effects. GABA and BDNF expression changes are observed but the causal chain to clinical anxiolysis is not fully established.

## Regulatory Status (as of May 2026)
- Current FDA category status: Not evaluated by FDA. Not on FDA bulk drug substance evaluation lists.
- 503A compoundability: Available through some 503A compounding pharmacies in the US; state-level variance applies
- 503B compoundability: Not available through 503B outsourcing facilities
- Recent regulatory changes: No specific FDA actions targeting Selank
- Pending FDA actions: None
- International: Approved as a medication in Russia (intranasal formulation). Not approved in any Western jurisdiction. Not specifically restricted by TGA or EMA.

## Clinical Considerations
- Theoretical benefits: Anxiolytic effects without sedation or dependence, cognitive support, immune modulation
- Known contraindications: Pregnancy (insufficient data), active autoimmune conditions (immunomodulatory concerns)
- Drug interactions: Theoretical interaction with benzodiazepines and other GABAergic drugs (additive GABA modulation). No formal interaction studies.
- Monitoring: Anxiety symptom tracking, cognitive assessment, immune markers (if using for immunomodulatory purposes)
- Population-specific: Patients seeking non-benzodiazepine anxiolytic options represent the primary interest group. The non-sedating, non-addictive profile is the primary appeal.

## Differentiation Angle
This monograph does what no existing Selank source does: it takes the Russian clinical approval seriously as evidence while being transparent about the evidentiary gap between Russian regulatory approval and Western clinical validation. Vendor pages cite Russian approval as if it settles the question. Western skeptics dismiss the research tradition entirely. Bloom acknowledges both the real-world clinical use (which is meaningful) and the absence of Western-standard trials (which is also meaningful).
