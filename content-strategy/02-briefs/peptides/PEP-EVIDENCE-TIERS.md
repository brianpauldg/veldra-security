## The Angle
The peptide information ecosystem lacks a consistent, transparent framework for rating evidence quality. Vendor pages cite preclinical data as if it were clinical proof. Skeptic sites dismiss everything without nuance. Bloom's four-tier evidence system provides a standardized, honestly calibrated framework that patients can use to evaluate any peptide claim — not just the peptides Bloom covers. This is a methodology document: it explains how Bloom rates evidence, why the tiers exist, and what each tier means for clinical confidence.

### H2: The Four Tiers

#### Tier 1 — Strong Human Clinical Evidence
- Multiple independent human clinical trials (Phase II/III)
- Replicated across research groups and institutions
- Published in peer-reviewed, PubMed-indexed journals
- Clear mechanism of action with human pharmacokinetic data
- Example context: FDA-approved medications, established clinical interventions

#### Tier 2 — Established Mechanism with Limited Human Data
- Well-characterized mechanism of action
- Some human pharmacokinetic or pharmacodynamic data exists
- Preclinical evidence is strong and independently replicated
- Clinical development may have begun but not completed Phase III
- Example peptides in this tier: MOTS-c, GHK-Cu, CJC-1295, Ipamorelin

#### Tier 3 — Strong Preclinical, No Human Trials
- Extensive animal model data, often from concentrated research groups
- Mechanism is plausible and internally consistent
- No completed human interventional trials
- May have significant anecdotal patient reports (which are noted but not weighted as clinical evidence)
- Example peptides in this tier: BPC-157, TB-500

#### Tier 4 — Intriguing Hypothesis, Limited Validation
- Research originates from limited sources (single lab, single country, single research tradition)
- Independent Western replication is sparse or absent
- The hypothesis may be scientifically plausible but the evidence is insufficient for clinical confidence
- May have regulatory approval in non-Western jurisdictions (which is noted as evidence of clinical use, not as Western-standard validation)
- Example peptides in this tier: Epitalon, Selank

### H2: How Bloom Applies the Tiers
- H3: Every monograph in the Bloom Peptide Library includes a tier rating with justification
- H3: Tier ratings are reviewed quarterly and may change as new evidence emerges
- H3: A lower tier does not mean "worthless" — it means "less clinical confidence"
- H3: A higher tier does not mean "safe and effective for you" — it means "more clinical data exists"

### H2: What the Tiers Do Not Do
- H3: They do not replace clinical judgment or provider consultation
- H3: They do not make commercial recommendations
- H3: They do not predict regulatory outcomes

### H2: How to Use This Framework When Evaluating Any Peptide Claim
- H3: Ask "what tier of evidence supports this claim?"
- H3: Ask "is the study human or animal?"
- H3: Ask "how many independent groups have replicated this?"
- H3: Ask "what don't we know?"
