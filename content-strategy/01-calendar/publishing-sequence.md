## Strategic Rationale

### Territory pillars first (Weeks 1-4)
The first four weeks establish all territory pillar anchors. By the end of week 4, Bloom has published the defining article for T-TERR-1 (Symptomatic Threshold), T-TERR-2 (Fertility-Preserving TRT), G-TERR-1 (Lean-Mass-Preserving GLP-1), G-TERR-3 (503A Compounding Standards), and P-TERR-2 (Peptide Regulatory Map). G-TERR-2 (Metabolic Set-Point Reset) follows in week 7. This front-loading ensures that every subsequent spoke article can reference its territory anchor from day one.

### Monograph cadence as content engine (Weeks 5-16)
The 8 monographs publish at a cadence of one per two weeks, interspersed with TRT and GLP-1 spoke articles. This is denser than the original peptide cadence because the monograph system is a content engine, not a collection of interspersed authority pieces. Each monograph is 3,500-5,000 words of deeply researched, honestly rated, regularly updated reference content. The cumulative effect over 16 weeks is a peptide reference library that no competitor can replicate quickly.

### Evidence Tier System precedes monographs (Week 3)
PEP-EVIDENCE-TIERS publishes before the first monograph so readers encounter the tier methodology before they encounter a tier rating. This prevents confusion and establishes the intellectual framework that gives the Library its rigor.

### Cross-pillar piece at week 12
CROSS-001 ships only after both TRT and GLP-1 pillars have established trust through 10+ articles each. The body composition combination argument requires credibility in both domains. Shipping it too early risks appearing promotional rather than evidence-based.

### TRT-011 as buffer/capstone
The comprehensive TRT guide publishes last by design. It links to every TRT article in the cluster. Publishing it before the cluster is complete wastes internal linking potential.

### Buyer-journey alignment preserved
Weeks 1-4 remain awareness-stage (territory pillar anchors, foundational concepts). Weeks 5-10 are consideration-stage (spokes, protocol details, mechanism). Weeks 11-16 are decision-stage and authority-building (cross-pillar, comparison, comprehensive guides, final monographs).
