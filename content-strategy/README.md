## What This Directory Is

This directory contains the complete content operations system for Bloom Metabolics. It houses article briefs, foundational reference documents, keyword architecture, compliance guardrails, and publishing workflows for all educational content across three pillars: Testosterone Optimization, Metabolic Health (GLP-1), and Peptide Science.

Every piece of content published under the /learn/ subdomain originates here. The briefs define the article scope. The foundation documents define how those articles are written, reviewed, and published.

This is not a brainstorming folder. It is an execution system.

## Why It Exists

Bloom Metabolics competes in YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) health content. Google applies heightened scrutiny to medical content. The FDA regulates how compounded medications are described. LegitScript monitors telehealth marketing claims. A single non-compliant article can delay certification, trigger platform removal, or erode trust with the exact audience we are trying to reach.

This system exists to make compliant, high-quality content production repeatable. Every article follows the same process. Every claim is backed. Every draft is reviewed before publication.

## How to Use It

### Workflow

1. **Select a brief.** Pick the next priority article from the publishing sequence (`01-calendar/publishing-sequence.md`). Briefs are stored in pillar subdirectories (`02-briefs/trt/`, `02-briefs/glp1/`, `02-briefs/peptides/`, `02-briefs/cross-pillar/`).

2. **Check territory assignment.** Every brief has a Territory and Role in territory field. Review the territory's named concept, terminology requirements, and the Territory Reinforcement section at the bottom of the brief.

3. **Run the article draft prompt.** Open the brief file. It contains the target keyword, semantic keyword map, article structure, source requirements, and internal linking map. Use this brief as input to generate the first draft.

4. **Apply voice and compliance filters.** Cross-reference the draft against `00-foundation/01-positioning-and-voice.md` for tone, `00-foundation/03-compliance-guardrails.md` for language compliance, and `00-foundation/06-concept-territories.md` for territory consistency.

5. **Medical reviewer signoff.** Route the draft to Dr. Napolitano, MD. No article publishes without clinical review signoff.

6. **Technical SEO pass.** Apply schema markup, heading structure, internal links, and meta descriptions per `00-foundation/04-technical-seo-checklist.md`.

7. **Territory terminology consistency check.** Verify that the territory's named concept appears with correct phrasing, no variant phrasings exist, and internal links to the territory pillar anchor are present.

8. **Publish.** Deploy the article to the /learn/ subdirectory. Update the status tracker below. Submit the URL to Google Search Console for indexing.

## Pre-Publish Checklist

Before any article moves from "Draft Complete" to "Published," confirm every item below:

- [ ] **Territory terminology consistency check.** The assigned territory concept appears with correct phrasing per the Territory Reinforcement section in the brief. No variant phrasings. Internal links to the territory pillar anchor are present.
- [ ] **Semantic keyword map fulfilled.** The latent semantic terms from the brief's Semantic Keyword Map appear naturally in the finished piece. Patient-search questions are addressed in H2/H3 subheads where natural.
- [ ] **Compliance review complete.** All language checked against `03-compliance-guardrails.md`. No prohibited phrasing. No absolute health claims. No named pharmacy partners.
- [ ] **Medical reviewer signoff.** Reviewer has read the final draft and approved. Reviewer name and credentials appear in the article byline or reviewer attribution block.
- [ ] **Schema markup applied.** MedicalWebPage, Article, and FAQPage (if applicable) JSON-LD blocks are present. Validate with Google Rich Results Test.
- [ ] **Internal linking complete.** 3-5 internal links per spoke article. At least one link to the parent pillar hub. At least one link to the territory pillar anchor. At least one contextual CTA link to the relevant service page (except peptide content — education-only, no CTAs).
- [ ] **Image alt text written.** Every image has descriptive, keyword-relevant alt text.
- [ ] **Meta description written.** 150-160 characters. Includes the primary keyword naturally.
- [ ] **Canonical URL set.** Self-referencing canonical tag.
- [ ] **Open Graph and Twitter Card meta tags present.**
- [ ] **Heading hierarchy valid.** One H1. H2s for major sections. H3s nest under H2s. No skipped levels.
- [ ] **URL slug follows convention.** Lowercase, hyphenated, no dates, no stop words.
- [ ] **Mobile rendering verified.**
- [ ] **Page speed acceptable.** LCP under 2.5 seconds. CLS under 0.1.
- [ ] **Author bio block present.** Linked to the author's Person schema.
- [ ] **"Last medically reviewed" date present.** Displayed above the fold.
- [ ] **Submitted to Google Search Console.** URL inspection tool, request indexing.

## How to Update the Calendar When Priorities Shift

Content priorities will change. A regulatory announcement may require fast-tracking a peptide monograph update. A competitor may publish something that demands a response.

When priorities shift:

1. **Move the new priority article to the top of the current sprint.** Note the reason.
2. **Update the status tracker** for any article in progress.
3. **Document the reason for the shift** in a commit message.
4. **Do not skip the compliance review** because of urgency.
5. **Do not skip the territory terminology check** — consistency is the compound interest of content authority.
