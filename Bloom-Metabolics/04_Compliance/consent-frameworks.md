# Consent Document Frameworks

## 1. Telehealth Informed Consent

**Purpose:** Establish informed consent for receiving healthcare via telehealth.

### Framework Elements

**Section A — Nature of Telehealth Services**
- Definition of telehealth (audio/video consultation, asynchronous messaging, remote monitoring)
- Services provided via telehealth (consultation, treatment planning, lab review, follow-up care, prescription management)
- How telehealth differs from in-person care
- Technology requirements (internet connection, device with camera/microphone, secure browser)

**Section B — Potential Benefits**
- Convenience and accessibility
- Reduced travel time and cost
- Access to specialists regardless of location
- Continuity of care through digital monitoring

**Section C — Potential Limitations and Risks**
- Technology failures may interrupt service
- Provider cannot perform physical examination
- Some conditions require in-person evaluation
- Information transmitted may not be sufficient for medical decision-making
- Delays in evaluation and treatment may occur

**Section D — Privacy and Confidentiality**
- HIPAA protections apply to telehealth encounters
- Encryption of all communications
- No recording of sessions without explicit consent
- Limitations of electronic communication security
- Third-party technology providers and their privacy practices

**Section E — Patient Rights**
- Right to refuse telehealth and request in-person care
- Right to withdraw consent at any time
- Right to request copy of medical records
- Right to know identity and credentials of provider
- Right to second opinion

**Section F — Emergency Procedures**
- Telehealth is NOT for medical emergencies
- If experiencing emergency, call 911 or go to nearest ER
- Provider cannot prescribe emergency medications via telehealth

**Section G — Provider Credentials**
- Provider name, degree, license number, state(s) of licensure
- DEA registration (for controlled substances)
- Board certifications

**Section H — Consent Acknowledgment**
- Patient signature (electronic) with date
- Patient confirms they have read and understand all sections
- Patient confirms they are located in [state] at time of consultation
- Patient confirms they are the individual seeking treatment (identity verification)

## 2. Financial Consent / Payment Authorization

### Framework Elements

**Section A — Subscription Terms**
- Selected plan and monthly price
- What is included (medication, labs at specified intervals, provider access, messaging)
- What is NOT included (emergency care, hospitalization, in-person visits)
- Billing cycle (monthly from date of first subscription)

**Section B — Payment Method**
- Authorization to charge provided payment method
- Automatic monthly renewal
- Notification of payment processing (receipt via email)

**Section C — Cancellation Policy**
- Cancel anytime from account settings or by contacting support
- Cancellation effective at end of current billing period
- No penalty for cancellation
- Remaining medication/supplies through end of paid period

**Section D — Refund Policy**
- Consultation fee refundable if patient does not qualify for treatment
- Subscription fees: [specify refund terms — pro-rata, full month, or no refund]
- Medication shipped is non-refundable

**Section E — Price Changes**
- 30-day notice for any price increases
- Patient may cancel before price change takes effect
- Grandfathering policy (if applicable)

**Section F — Failed Payment**
- Automatic retry after 3 and 5 days
- Email notification of failed payment
- Account paused after [X] days of failed payment
- Treatment continuity plan during billing issues

**Section G — Insurance Disclaimer**
- Services are cash-pay only
- Bloom Metabolics does not bill insurance
- Patient may submit receipts to insurance for potential reimbursement (not guaranteed)
- FSA/HSA may be eligible (patient should verify with their plan)

## 3. Communication Consent

### Framework Elements

**Section A — Communication Types**
- Clinical communications (lab results, appointment reminders, treatment updates)
- Operational communications (billing notifications, account updates)
- Marketing communications (educational content, service updates, promotions)

**Section B — Communication Channels**
- Email: [patient email]
- SMS/Text: [patient phone number]
- Phone: [patient phone number]
- In-app messaging (secure, HIPAA-compliant)

**Section C — Consent Specifics**
- Consent to receive clinical communications via email and SMS (required for care)
- Consent to receive operational communications via email (required for service)
- Consent to receive marketing communications via email (optional, opt-in)
- Consent to receive SMS marketing (optional, opt-in, per TCPA requirements)

**Section D — Opt-Out Procedures**
- Unsubscribe from marketing emails via link in every email (per CAN-SPAM)
- Reply STOP to opt out of marketing SMS (per TCPA)
- Clinical and operational communications cannot be opted out while receiving treatment
- Contact support to modify communication preferences

**Section E — PHI in Communications**
- Secure messaging used for PHI (lab results, clinical questions)
- Email and SMS may contain appointment reminders but NOT detailed PHI
- Patient acknowledges risk of email/SMS interception

## 4. Lab Consent

### Framework Elements

**Section A — Authorization**
- Patient authorizes provider to order laboratory tests
- Tests ordered based on clinical necessity and treatment protocol
- Patient understands which tests are being ordered and general purpose

**Section B — Lab Provider**
- Labs performed by [Quest Diagnostics / Labcorp / specified partner]
- Patient may choose lab location for convenience
- Lab partner's own consent and privacy policies apply

**Section C — Fasting and Preparation**
- Some tests require fasting (12 hours, water only)
- Specific preparation instructions provided with lab requisition
- Patient acknowledges responsibility for following preparation instructions

**Section D — Results Communication**
- Results typically available within 1-3 business days
- Provider reviews all results before releasing to patient
- Critical values communicated within 24 hours
- Routine results communicated within 48 hours
- Results accessible via patient portal

**Section E — Abnormal Results**
- Provider will contact patient to discuss abnormal results
- Additional testing may be recommended
- Referral to specialist if clinically appropriate
- Patient understands right to seek second opinion

## 5. Medication Acknowledgement

### Framework Elements

**Section A — Medication Information**
- Name of prescribed medication
- Purpose and expected benefits
- Dosage, frequency, and administration method
- Expected timeline for effects

**Section B — Compounded Medication Disclosure** *(if applicable)*
- "This medication is compounded by a licensed pharmacy. It is NOT an FDA-approved medication."
- "Compounded medications are prepared specifically for you based on your provider's prescription."
- "Compounded medications have not undergone FDA review for safety, efficacy, or manufacturing standards."
- "Your provider has determined that a compounded medication is appropriate for your individual needs."

**Section C — Potential Side Effects**
- Common side effects (listed by medication type)
- Serious side effects requiring immediate medical attention
- When to contact provider vs. when to seek emergency care

**Section D — Reporting Adverse Effects**
- How to report side effects (secure messaging, phone, portal)
- Expected response time from provider
- When to go to ER (severe allergic reaction, chest pain, difficulty breathing)

**Section E — Refill Process**
- Automatic refill reminders at [X] days before supply runs out
- Patient confirms shipping address and reports any issues
- Provider approval required for continued refills
- Refill may be held pending scheduled labs or check-in

**Section F — Storage and Handling**
- Storage instructions (temperature, light exposure)
- Disposal instructions
- Keep out of reach of children
- Do not share medication with others

**Section G — Drug Interactions**
- Patient must inform provider of all current medications, supplements, and OTC drugs
- Specific interaction warnings (listed by medication type)
- Patient must contact provider before starting any new medication

## 6. Privacy Notice (HIPAA-Compliant)

### Framework Elements

**Section A — Information We Collect**
- Personal identifiers (name, DOB, address, email, phone)
- Medical information (history, symptoms, lab results, medications, vitals)
- Payment information (processed via Stripe, not stored by us)
- Usage data (website analytics, device info, IP address)
- Communication records (messages, emails, call logs)

**Section B — How We Use Your Information**
- Providing medical care and treatment
- Processing payments and managing subscriptions
- Communicating about your care (appointments, results, refills)
- Quality improvement and clinical outcome tracking
- Legal and regulatory compliance
- De-identified data for research and analytics (with consent)

**Section C — Who We Share Information With**
- Your treating provider(s)
- Laboratory partners (for test ordering and results)
- Pharmacy partners (for prescription fulfillment)
- Payment processors (Stripe — payment data only)
- Technology partners with BAAs (database, communication platforms)
- As required by law (court orders, public health reporting)
- We do NOT sell your personal or medical information

**Section D — Your Rights**
- Access: Request copy of your medical records
- Correction: Request amendment of inaccurate information
- Deletion: Request deletion of your data (subject to retention requirements)
- Restriction: Request limits on how we use your data
- Portability: Receive your data in a standard format
- Complaint: File complaint with HHS Office for Civil Rights

**Section E — Data Security**
- AES-256-GCM encryption for sensitive medical data
- TLS 1.3 for all data in transit
- Role-based access controls
- Regular security assessments
- Audit logging of all data access

**Section F — Data Retention**
- Medical records retained for minimum [7-10 years, per state law]
- Payment records retained for [7 years, per tax requirements]
- Account data deleted upon request (subject to retention requirements)
- Audit logs retained for [minimum 6 years per HIPAA]

**Section G — Breach Notification**
- We will notify affected individuals within 60 days of discovering a breach (per HIPAA)
- Notification will include: what happened, what information was involved, what we are doing, what you can do
- Breaches affecting 500+ individuals reported to HHS and media

**Section H — Contact Information**
- Privacy Officer: [Name, email, phone]
- Mailing address for privacy inquiries
- HHS Office for Civil Rights complaint process

## Consent Collection Flow

### At Booking (Pre-Payment)
1. ☐ Terms of Service agreement
2. ☐ Privacy Notice acknowledgment
3. ☐ Communication consent (clinical + operational)
4. ☐ Financial consent / payment authorization

### At Intake (Post-Payment, Pre-Consultation)
5. ☐ Telehealth informed consent
6. ☐ Medical information accuracy attestation
7. ☐ Marketing communication consent (optional)

### At Treatment Start (Post-Consultation)
8. ☐ Lab consent
9. ☐ Medication acknowledgement (specific to prescribed medication)
10. ☐ Controlled substance acknowledgement (if TRT)

### Storage
- All consents stored with: patient ID, timestamp, IP address, consent version
- Consents are immutable — new consent replaces old, but old version retained
- Audit log entry created for each consent action
