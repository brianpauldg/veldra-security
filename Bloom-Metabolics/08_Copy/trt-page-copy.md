## What Is TRT

**Headline:** Testosterone therapy — the science, simply explained.

Testosterone is the primary hormone responsible for energy, muscle development, fat metabolism, mood regulation, cognitive sharpness, and sexual health in men. Production naturally declines after age 30, typically by 1-2% per year.

For many men, this gradual decline leads to symptoms that significantly impact quality of life. Testosterone replacement therapy (TRT) restores testosterone to optimal levels through physician-prescribed protocols — typically injectable testosterone cypionate, administered weekly or biweekly.

TRT is not a performance enhancer. It's a restoration of what your body used to produce on its own. When monitored with comprehensive labs and managed by an experienced provider, TRT may help support energy levels, body composition, mood stability, mental clarity, and overall vitality.

*Individual results vary. TRT is prescribed by licensed providers based on lab results and clinical evaluation.*

## FAQ

**Q: What is TRT exactly?**
A: Testosterone replacement therapy restores testosterone to optimal levels using physician-prescribed medication — typically injectable testosterone cypionate. It's designed for men whose natural testosterone production has declined below the range associated with healthy function.

**Q: Am I a candidate for TRT?**
A: Candidacy is determined by your lab results and clinical evaluation. Generally, men with symptoms of low testosterone and bloodwork confirming suboptimal levels may be candidates. Take our free assessment to get started, and your provider will make the final determination.

**Q: What lab tests do you order?**
A: Our comprehensive panel includes: total testosterone, free testosterone, estradiol (E2), PSA, hematocrit, hemoglobin, SHBG, LH, FSH, prolactin, lipid panel, comprehensive metabolic panel, liver function, thyroid (TSH, free T3, free T4), and complete blood count.

**Q: Is testosterone a controlled substance?**
A: Yes. Testosterone is classified as a Schedule III controlled substance. It requires a prescription from a licensed provider with DEA registration. All prescriptions are sent electronically to licensed pharmacies.

**Q: Will TRT affect my fertility?**
A: TRT may reduce sperm production. If you're planning to have children, discuss this with your provider during consultation. Alternative protocols or adjunct medications (like HCG) may be considered.

**Q: How soon will I notice results?**
A: Individual responses vary. Many patients report initial improvements in energy and mood within 2-4 weeks. Body composition changes typically become noticeable over 3-6 months. Your provider will monitor your progress through regular check-ins and lab work.

**Q: What are the potential side effects?**
A: Common side effects may include acne, fluid retention, and increased red blood cell count. Less common effects include mood changes and injection site reactions. Your provider monitors for these through regular bloodwork and adjusts your protocol as needed.

**Q: Do you prescribe creams or gels?**
A: Our primary protocol uses injectable testosterone cypionate, which is generally considered the most effective and consistent delivery method. If you have a preference for topical application, discuss this with your provider.

**Q: What if my current doctor says my levels are "normal"?**
A: "Normal" reference ranges are very broad (typically 264-916 ng/dL) and don't account for symptoms or individual optimal levels. A man at 300 ng/dL is technically "normal" but may be significantly below his optimal range. We focus on optimal levels, not just reference ranges.

**Q: Can I cancel if TRT isn't right for me?**
A: Absolutely. Cancel anytime from your account. No contracts, no penalties, no hidden fees. If you're within your first month and TRT isn't recommended after lab review, your consultation fee is refunded.
