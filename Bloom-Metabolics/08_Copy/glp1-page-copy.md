## The Science

**Headline:** How GLP-1 therapy works — and why it's different.

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonists are a class of medications originally developed for type 2 diabetes that have shown significant effects on weight management. Medications like semaglutide work by:

**Reducing appetite** — GLP-1 acts on brain receptors that regulate hunger and satiety, helping you feel satisfied with less food without the constant willpower battle.

**Slowing gastric emptying** — Food moves through your stomach more slowly, keeping you fuller longer after meals.

**Supporting insulin function** — GLP-1 helps your body process glucose more efficiently, supporting healthier metabolic function.

**Targeting metabolic pathways** — Beyond appetite, GLP-1 therapy may support improvements in metabolic markers including blood sugar, lipids, and inflammatory markers.

This isn't a diet pill. It's a physician-managed protocol that works with your body's natural systems to support sustainable weight optimization.

*Individual results vary. GLP-1 medications are prescribed by licensed providers based on clinical evaluation.*

## FAQ

**Q: Who is a candidate for GLP-1 therapy?**
A: GLP-1 therapy may be appropriate for adults with a BMI of 27 or higher, or those with metabolic health concerns. Your provider will determine candidacy based on your labs, health history, and clinical evaluation during your consultation.

**Q: What medication do you prescribe?**
A: We primarily prescribe compounded semaglutide, which is the same active ingredient found in Ozempic and Wegovy. Compounded medications are prepared by licensed pharmacies specifically for your prescription. Your provider will discuss options and answer questions during your consultation.

**Q: Is compounded semaglutide the same as Ozempic or Wegovy?**
A: Compounded semaglutide contains the same active ingredient but is prepared by a compounding pharmacy, not the brand-name manufacturer. It is not FDA-approved and has not undergone the same review process. Your provider will explain the specifics during consultation.

**Q: What are common side effects?**
A: The most common side effects include nausea (especially during dose increases), decreased appetite, and mild digestive changes. These typically improve as your body adjusts. Your provider manages side effects through careful dose titration and regular check-ins.

**Q: How much weight can I expect to lose?**
A: Weight loss results vary significantly between individuals depending on starting weight, metabolism, medication response, diet, and activity level. Clinical studies of semaglutide have shown a range of results. Your provider can discuss realistic expectations based on your specific situation.

**Q: How long do I need to take GLP-1 medication?**
A: Treatment duration varies. Some patients use GLP-1 therapy for 6-12 months to reach their goals, then transition to maintenance strategies. Others benefit from longer-term use. Your provider will help you develop a plan based on your progress and goals.

**Q: Will I gain the weight back if I stop?**
A: Weight regain is possible after discontinuing any weight management medication. Our approach focuses on building sustainable habits alongside medication, so you have the best foundation for maintaining results. Your provider will develop a transition plan when you're ready.

**Q: Can I take GLP-1 with other medications?**
A: Your provider reviews all current medications during consultation to check for interactions. GLP-1 therapy may interact with certain diabetes medications, and dosing adjustments may be needed. Full medication disclosure during intake is essential.

**Q: Do I need to diet and exercise too?**
A: GLP-1 therapy works best as part of a comprehensive approach. While the medication supports appetite control and metabolic function, combining it with mindful nutrition and regular activity typically leads to better and more sustainable results. Our GLP-1 Complete plan includes nutrition guidance for this reason.

**Q: Is this covered by insurance?**
A: Bloom Metabolics operates on a cash-pay model. We do not bill insurance directly. If you're interested in insurance-covered brand-name GLP-1 medications (Wegovy, Zepbound), discuss this with your provider — we may be able to help coordinate.
