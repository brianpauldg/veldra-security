## MEDVi — Primary Competitor Deep Dive

### Overview
Fastest-growing telehealth company in the US. Founded by Matthew Gallagher with $20K and 2 employees. $401M in 2025 sales, tracking $1.8B in 2026. No outside funding. (Source: NYT, April 2, 2026)

### Service Lines
- GLP-1 Weight Loss (core revenue): Compounded semaglutide injections/tablets, branded Wegovy/Zepbound
- Men's Health (QUAD): Compounded ED formula
- Women's Health: Hormones, weight, hair, skin
- Peptide Therapy: Recovery, performance, longevity
- Hair Restoration, Skincare, Supplementation
- MEDVi Meals: Chef-prepared, medically aligned meal delivery

### Pricing
| Product | Price |
|---------|-------|
| Compounded semaglutide injection (Month 1) | $179 |
| Compounded semaglutide injection (Refills) | $299/mo |
| Compounded oral semaglutide tablet | $249/mo |
| Branded Wegovy/Zepbound | $99 membership + med cost |
| Branded Ozempic | $1,999/mo |

### Growth Engine
- 5,000+ active Meta Ads at peak (dropped to ~2,800 after scrutiny)
- Aggressive affiliate marketing network
- Heavy PR (Bloomberg, Forbes, NYT, Fortune, Fast Company)
- Quiz-first intake — no video consult barrier
- "500,000+ MEDVi patients" social proof claim
- AI-generated marketing content and customer service

### What MEDVi Does Well
- Category-leading pricing on compounded GLP-1 ($179 first month)
- Frictionless quiz-to-prescription flow (no video call)
- Massive scale with minimal headcount via AI
- Multi-product expansion (meals, men's health, peptides)
- Strong promotional cadence creating urgency

### What MEDVi Does Poorly / Legal Risks
- **FDA Warning Letter (Feb 20, 2026):** Misbranding violations — falsely suggesting MEDVi compounds its own semaglutide; implying FDA approval of compounded products
- **Class Action Lawsuit (March 20, 2026):** Deceptive spam emails, spoofed domains, falsified headers via affiliate marketers
- **Fake Doctor Ads:** Fabricated physician personas including "Professor Albust Dongledore" with AI-generated profiles
- **No meaningful clinical relationship** — pure prescription fulfillment
- **ConsumerAffairs: 3.6/5** (vs Trustpilot 4.4/5) — gap raises review management concerns
- Auto-renewal/cancellation complaints, refund term shifting

## What Must Be Avoided (Compliance Red Lines)

1. **NEVER** imply compounded meds are FDA-approved or equivalent to brand-name
2. **NEVER** use AI-generated physician personas in advertising
3. **NEVER** use affiliate marketers with uncontrolled messaging
4. **NEVER** claim to compound your own medications
5. **NEVER** make specific weight loss guarantees with numbers
6. **NEVER** position AI as making medical/prescribing decisions
7. **NEVER** use auto-renewal dark patterns
8. **ALWAYS** disclose compounded medication status clearly
9. **ALWAYS** use qualified health claims ("may support," "commonly report")
10. **ALWAYS** maintain clear separation between AI operations and clinical decisions
